## Key Strategic Shift: B2C to B2B

The team is moving away from B2C applications towards B2B solutions to build a **defensive MOAT** (source: Spatial AI Synch - May 07.txt). The core strategy involves:
- **Avoiding Big Tech Competition**: Acknowledging that the team cannot compete with Google or Meta on general-purpose assistants.
- **Vertical Specialization**: Finding niche, high-value problems that Big Tech is unlikely to address directly.
- **Local Penetration**: Using the Pakistani market (e.g., textile, medical, GSK, Abbott) for initial data gathering and testing before scaling globally.

## Technical Approach

- **On-Device Vision**: Focus on real-time "Image Understanding" with near-zero latency using on-device processing (source: Spatial AI Synch - May 07.txt).
- **Tablet First Strategy**: Usama suggested identifying organizations already using tablets and building solutions for them first, before transitioning to smart glasses or headsets.

## Specific B2B Use Case Leads

1. **Medical Rehabilitation**: AR/VR-based programs for stroke and spinal cord injury recovery to reduce therapist labor and provide data dashboards (source: Spatial AI Synch - May 07.txt).
2. **Infrastructure Maintenance**: Step-by-step guides for utility maintenance including hazard identification.
3. **High-Stakes Environments**: Scene understanding and data overlay for factories, warehouses, and data centers.
4. **Kitchen Operations**: AR assistants for large-scale kitchens (e.g., hospital kitchens) to manage fast-paced, complex environments.

## Next Steps: Evaluation Framework

The team will consolidate 15-20 ideas and evaluate them using an LLM-based scoring system based on specific [[b2b-evaluation-criteria]].
