# Loss-Control Walk-Throughs with Spatial Evidence and Hazard Scoring

![[problem_5_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_5_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: AR-guided walk-throughs spatially capture key areas and auto-measure clearances to generate standardized hazard scores and evidence reports for risk engineers.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Risk engineers must assess sites (sprinklers, electrical rooms, housekeeping, egress, storage heights) and write reports quickly. Consistency is hard; proof matters after a loss.
AR-guided walk with hazard prompts, spatial capture of key areas; auto-measuring clearances would resolve all this. We can also use this to generate standardized evidence reports. There are cross-facility hazard patterns + claims outcomes that can be used to train our system.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Automated spatial hazard detection (e.g., blocked egress, inadequate clearance), computer vision for compliance checking, cross-facility pattern recognition.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: Real-time AR prompts guiding the risk engineer to key inspection points, visual highlighting of detected hazards, and automated on-screen measurements (e.g., clearance distance).

## Grounding
The industrial sector in Pakistan (textiles, manufacturing) faces frequent fire hazards and safety incidents. Insurance companies struggle with standardized risk assessment and fraudulent claims. Providing local insurers (EFU, Adamjee, Jubilee) with a spatial tool ensures objective, tamper-proof assessments of industrial sites, leading to better premium pricing and loss prevention.

## MOAT
The insurance data moat is uniquely powerful because it bridges two data domains that are almost never connected: (a) spatial hazard observations from physical sites and (b) financial claims outcomes. No tech company alone has claims data; no insurer alone has spatially-indexed hazard data at scale. The platform that connects these two creates an un-replicable risk intelligence engine.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (8)**: Standardizes a highly subjective process and provides indisputable spatial evidence.
- **Adoption Criteria (7)**: Insurers are motivated to reduce payouts, but digitizing the traditional inspector workflow requires change management.
- **Moat Confidence (9)**: The combination of proprietary spatial hazard data linked to actual financial claims creates a highly unique and defensible intelligence asset.
