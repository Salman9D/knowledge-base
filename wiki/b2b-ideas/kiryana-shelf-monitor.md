# Kiryana Store Shelf-Stock Monitor

**Summary**: Phone-based scanning for Kiryana store owners to identify low stock and suggest orders to distributors.

**Sources**: [[pakistan-b2b-ideas-20]]

---

## Description
A simple phone app for the 1M+ Kiryana stores in Pakistan. The owner pans their phone over the shelves; the vision model identifies out-of-stock items (Dalda, Lipton, Lux) and places a draft order to the distributor.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Acts as a real-time "expert eye," using computer vision to scan store shelves and identify out-of-stock items or misplaced products by recognizing local packaging variants.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: Provides phone-based visual guidance, highlighting empty shelf spaces and overlaying restocking suggestions and "tap-to-order" buttons directly on the screen.

## Grounding
Distribution companies (Unilever, P&G) struggle with "last-mile visibility." They don't know what is actually on the shelf.

## MOAT
The largest visual SKU dataset of Pakistani grocery brands and packaging variants.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (7)**: Useful for inventory, though Kiryana owners are used to manual "guesstimation."
- **Adoption Criteria (8)**: Huge potential for partnership with FMCG giants who will subsidize the app for owners.
- **Moat Confidence (7)**: Moderate; Big Tech can do SKU recognition, but the "Pakistani brand" catalog is a significant data effort.
