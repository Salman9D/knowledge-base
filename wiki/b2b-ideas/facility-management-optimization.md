# Facility Management & Space Optimization

![[problem_7_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_7_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: Spatial AI ingests diverse building data to create occupancy-aware digital twins for VR-based scenario planning and proactive space optimization.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Many enterprises operate massive campuses at 40-60% hybrid utilization. They are paying for space they do not use. Facility management is reactive. Understanding how space is actually used (vs. how it was designed) requires expensive sensor retrofits.
Spatial AI ingests existing CCTV, BMS, access control, and WiFi data to build occupancy-aware digital twins of campuses. No new hardware would be required initially. VR-based scenario planning can be applied. For example, "If we consolidate Building 3 and reconfigure Building 7, what happens to foot traffic, HVAC load, cafeteria queuing?"

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Ingests and correlates multi-modal data (CCTV, WiFi) to track spatial utilization, predictive modeling of HVAC and foot traffic based on occupancy.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: VR environments for facility managers to simulate layout changes and view historical heatmaps of space utilization over a 3D campus model.

## Grounding
Large corporate campuses, university grounds (LUMS, NUST), and tech parks in Pakistan often struggle with energy inefficiency and space underutilization. With rising electricity costs, tools that can model HVAC loads and optimize space based on existing CCTV/WiFi data (without expensive new IoT sensors) provide an immediate financial use case for enterprise operators.

## MOAT
The moat is primarily longitudinal behavioral data (which takes time to accumulate and cannot be synthesized) combined with integration complexity (which creates switching costs). The moat strengthens significantly when the system spans multiple facilities for the same enterprise and when scenario predictions are validated against real outcomes. This is a slower-building but very sticky moat.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (7)**: Good cost-saving tool, though slightly less critical than operations or maintenance.
- **Adoption Criteria (7)**: No new hardware required is a big plus. Sales cycle with enterprise admin/facilities teams might be slow.
- **Moat Confidence (8)**: Sticky once integrated with legacy BMS/CCTV systems, and behavioral data over time is hard to replace.
