# Safety Platform for Underground Mines

![[problem_11_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_11_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: A SLAM-based hardware and software platform for underground mines to create digital twins, integrate IoT sensors, and model longitudinal hazards.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Underground mines (coal, metal, limestone) are high-risk environments where traditional safety measures are often reactive and mapping is labor-intensive. These environments lack GPS and have limited connectivity, making real-time monitoring difficult. As excavation progresses, the physical environment changes rapidly, often outpacing manual documentation and safety assessments.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Uses specialized hardware to generate real-time 3D digital twins of the mine as it is excavated, ensuring up-to-date spatial records.
- **IoT Integration**: Maps data from air quality, temperature, and structural integrity sensors directly onto the 3D spatial model for context-aware safety monitoring.
- **Hazard Modeling**: Analyzes historical spatial data to identify patterns in structural shifts or gas accumulations, providing predictive alerts for potential hazards.

## Grounding
Pakistan possesses significant mineral wealth, with large-scale mining for coal, salt (Khewra), and metals. However, the industry is plagued by low safety standards and frequent accidents. A physically grounded digital twin platform can bridge the gap between hazardous on-site conditions and remote safety management, providing an essential tool for modernization in a high-stakes local industry.

## MOAT
The moat is built on physically grounded data from unique, high-risk underground environments that are difficult for generic AI models to access. By capturing longitudinal hazard data (pre- and post-incident scans), the system develops unique predictive models for structural failure and gas leaks. Deep integration with specialized SLAM hardware further reinforces the platform's defensibility.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (9)**: Addresses critical life-safety issues in one of the world's most dangerous professions with advanced spatial technology.
- **Adoption Criteria (7)**: Large mining companies have high ROI for safety improvements, though hardware costs and ruggedization requirements are significant barriers.
- **Moat Confidence (9)**: Extremely high due to the difficulty of gathering data in these restricted, non-standardized environments.
