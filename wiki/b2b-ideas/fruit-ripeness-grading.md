# Cold-Chain Fruit Ripeness Vision (Mango/Kinnow)

**Summary**: Vision assistant at sorting stations to classify export-grade fruit by ripeness and surface defects.

**Sources**: [[pakistan-b2b-ideas-20]]

---

## Description
Pakistan exports Mangoes and Kinnows globally. Over-ripe fruit in a container can ruin the whole shipment. A fixed camera rig above sorting lines uses vision models to classify fruit by "days-to-overripe" based on skin color and texture.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Acts as a real-time "expert eye," using computer vision to analyze skin color, texture, and surface defects to scientifically grade fruit ripeness and estimate remaining shelf-life.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: Provides tablet-first or station-fixed visual feedback, color-coding fruit by ripeness level on the live feed and flagging "export-grade" vs. "local-market" items automatically.

## Grounding
Post-harvest losses in Pakistan are ~30-40%. Export rejection due to "shelf-life failure" is a major pain point.

## MOAT
Ripening data for specific local cultivars (Sindhri, Chaunsa, Sargodha Kinnow).

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (9)**: Scientific ripeness grading is vastly superior to manual "pressing" by workers.
- **Adoption Criteria (8)**: Large export houses (e.g., Lutfabad, Iftekhar Ahmed & Co) have the budget for tech that saves containers.
- **Moat Confidence (8)**: Strong; local fruit varieties have unique visual signatures.
