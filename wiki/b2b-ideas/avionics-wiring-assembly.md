# Avionics Wiring Harness Assembly for Commercial Satellites & Spacecraft

![[problem_19_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_19_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: Assisting in the complex assembly of avionics wiring harnesses for satellites using holographic trails and pin-level verification.

## Description
Assembling avionics wiring harnesses for satellites and spacecraft involves routing thousands of individual wires through tight, complex paths. A single misrouted wire or incorrect pin placement can lead to mission failure. Currently, technicians rely on complex paper diagrams and "wiring boards," making the process slow and high-risk.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **Holographic Wiring Trails**: Projects a 3D "trail" for every wire, showing the technician exactly where to route it through the spacecraft's chassis.
- **Pin Verification**: Uses computer vision to verify that each wire is inserted into the correct pin on a connector, providing real-time error detection.
- **CAD-to-Reality Mapping**: Automatically translates complex 3D CAD wiring designs into interactive, on-site AR instructions.

## Grounding
Pakistan's space program (SUPARCO) and the growing interest in private satellite development require high-precision manufacturing capabilities. This technology allows local engineers to perform aerospace-grade assembly with higher speed and lower error rates, supporting the country's ambitions in the global space economy.

## MOAT
The moat is built on CAD-to-reality mapping, temporal error-correction data (learning from common mistakes), and proprietary assembly telemetry. The ability to integrate deeply with aerospace CAD software and capture the "human-in-the-loop" assembly data creates a defensible technical advantage that improves over time.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (9)**: Dramatic reduction in errors and assembly time for high-value, complex electronic systems.
- **Adoption Criteria (8)**: High ROI in aerospace manufacturing where the cost of a single error is catastrophic.
- **Moat Confidence (8)**: Strong defensibility through deep software integration and proprietary assembly datasets.
