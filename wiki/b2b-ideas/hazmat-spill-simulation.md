# Spatial Simulation for HazMat Spill Containment in Chemical Plants

![[problem_25_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_25_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: Dynamic physics-based simulation of gas clouds and hazardous spills for high-stakes emergency response training in chemical facilities.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Traditional "tabletop" exercises for hazardous material (HazMat) spills lack the visceral reality of a real emergency. In a chemical plant, understanding the invisible movement of toxic gas clouds or the rapid spread of a liquid spill is a matter of life and death. 
Dynamic physics-based simulations allow personnel to train for "fatal exposure" scenarios in a safe, immersive environment, using the actual facility layout as the training ground.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Orchestrates dynamic chemical-dispersal physics engines that respond to environmental factors like wind speed, humidity, and facility geometry. It tracks trainee movements to calculate "simulated exposure" levels in real-time.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: Visualizes invisible hazards (e.g., colorless toxic gases) directly within the physical facility or a high-fidelity digital twin. Trainees must navigate the site to shut down valves and contain the spill while avoiding the simulated gas clouds.

## Grounding
Safety compliance and risk reduction are top priorities for chemical and petrochemical manufacturers. Improving the effectiveness of emergency response training can prevent catastrophic accidents and reduce insurance liabilities.

## MOAT
Proprietary chemical-dispersal physics engines tuned for specific industrial layouts and a "risk-readiness" metric derived from longitudinal training data across different facility types.
