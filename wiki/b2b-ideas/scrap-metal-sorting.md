# Scrap Metal Sorting Vision (Gadani/Port Qasim)

**Summary**: Vision-based identification of metal types (copper, aluminum, steel) in mixed scrap for ship-breaking and foundries.

**Sources**: [[pakistan-b2b-ideas-20]]

---

## Description
A tablet/camera rig at scrap yards. It uses vision models trained on metal textures and oxidation patterns to sort high-value non-ferrous metals from common steel scrap.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Acts as a real-time "expert eye," using computer vision to analyze metal textures and oxidation patterns, automatically classifying scrap into high-value types like copper, aluminum, or brass.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: Provides tablet-first visual feedback, color-coding different metal types on the live feed to guide workers in sorting and maximizing the recovered value from mixed scrap loads.

## Grounding
Pakistan has a huge ship-breaking industry (Gadani) and steel re-rolling sector. Manual sorting often misses high-value copper or brass.

## MOAT
Visual data on aged/corroded marine-grade metals specific to Gadani's scrap mix.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (8)**: Prevents "lost value" in mixed scrap loads.
- **Adoption Criteria (7)**: Gadani is a rough environment, but the financial gain of catching 10kg of copper is high.
- **Moat Confidence (9)**: Marine scrap has unique visual characteristics compared to industrial scrap.
