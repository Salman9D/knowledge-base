# Remote Expert Assistance for Power & Utility Infrastructure Maintenance

![[problem_1_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_1_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: MR headsets allow remote experts to spatially annotate the live environment of field technicians maintaining utility infrastructure.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Maintenance of substations, transformers, and transmission towers is performed by field technicians with varying skill levels across remote locations. Expert engineers are concentrated in metros. Downtime costs can be really high for critical substations.
MR headset or phone-based AR system would let a remote expert spatially annotate the field technician's live environment. The system would recognize equipment (transformer bushings, breaker panels, relay configurations) and pull up relevant SOPs, historical fault data, and wiring diagrams registered to the physical object. Session recordings become spatially-indexed knowledge bases for future technicians and senior managers.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Object recognition of utility equipment (bushings, panels, relays), anomaly detection for visible wear or faults, contextual retrieval of historical fault data and SOPs.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: 3D spatial annotations by remote experts, overlaid wiring diagrams registered directly onto physical panels, step-by-step visual guidance for fault isolation.

## Grounding
In Pakistan, technical expertise is heavily centralized in cities like Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad, while critical power infrastructure (WAPDA, K-Electric, NTDC grids) is spread across vast remote areas. Frequent power outages and maintenance delays are common. An AR-based remote assistance tool can drastically reduce downtime and travel costs by allowing a single expert to guide multiple on-site technicians across the country in real-time.

## MOAT
The data moat is multi-layered and compounding. It combines (a) proprietary spatial visual data of physical infrastructure, (b) expert procedural knowledge captured in spatial context, (c) longitudinal fault/resolution histories, and (d) cross-customer equipment recognition breadth. A competitor entering after 2 years of our operation would need to generate thousands of spatially-annotated field sessions to even approach feature parity.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (8)**: Direct ROI through reduced downtime and travel costs. It bridges a critical skill gap in real-time.
- **Adoption Criteria (7)**: High utility, but hardware costs (headsets) and reliable internet connectivity in remote areas of Pakistan could pose challenges. Phone-based AR might see faster initial adoption.
- **Moat Confidence (9)**: Compounding data moat. Collecting specialized equipment visual data and mapping it to procedural resolutions over time creates an incredibly high barrier to entry.
