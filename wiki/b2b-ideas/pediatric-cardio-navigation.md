# 3D Navigation for Pediatric Cardiothoracic Surgery

![[problem_20_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_20_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: 3D holographic navigation for scalpel guidance in pediatric heart surgeries for congenital defects, using real-time beating-heart models.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Pediatric cardiothoracic surgery, particularly for congenital heart defects, is among the most challenging medical procedures. Surgeons must operate on hearts the size of a walnut, where spatial margins are measured in millimeters. Conventional 2D imaging (ultrasound, CT) requires the surgeon to perform a "mental rotation" to understand the 3D anatomy.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **Holographic Heart Model**: Projects a patient-specific, 3D holographic model of the beating heart that the surgeon can view through AR glasses during the operation.
- **Scalpel Navigation**: Provides real-time spatial guidance for the scalpel, highlighting critical structures (like tiny arteries or valves) that must be avoided or repaired.
- **Pre-Surgical Simulation**: Allows surgeons to "practice" the procedure on a 3D digital twin of the patient's heart before the first incision is made.

## Grounding
Pakistan has a high incidence of congenital heart disease (CHD), and facilities like NICVD are world-renowned for their high volume of pediatric cases. Implementing 3D navigation can significantly improve outcomes and reduce surgical time in these high-pressure, high-volume environments, making advanced cardiac care more accessible and effective.

## MOAT
The moat is centered on unique pediatric anatomical models, deep clinical partnerships with leading heart institutes, and a restricted dataset of pediatric cardiac outcomes. Training AI to accurately map and predict the dynamics of a small, diseased, beating heart is a massive technical challenge that requires years of specialized surgical data.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (9)**: Provides transformative spatial visualization and guidance for life-saving surgeries.
- **Adoption Criteria (7)**: Requires high levels of clinical validation and integration into sterile surgical environments.
- **Moat Confidence (9)**: Extremely high due to the specialized nature of the medical data and the deep expertise required to build surgical-grade models.
