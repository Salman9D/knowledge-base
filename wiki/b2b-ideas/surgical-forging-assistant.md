# Surgical Instrument Material Forging Assistant

**Summary**: Vision-based heat and color analysis for manual forging of surgical steel to ensure metallurgical consistency.

**Sources**: [[pakistan-b2b-ideas-20]], [[b2b-evaluation-criteria]]

---

## Description
A tablet-mounted system that uses specialized vision models to analyze the color temperature of steel during the manual forging process. It guides the blacksmith on the exact moment to quench or strike, ensuring the final instrument meets international tensile strength standards.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Acts as a real-time "expert eye," using computer vision to analyze the color temperature of forging steel, identifying the precise metallurgical moment for quenching or striking.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: Provides tablet-first visual feedback, displaying real-time temperature data and "strike now" indicators directly over the glowing steel in the blacksmith's view.

## Grounding
Sialkot's surgical industry relies heavily on manual forging. Inconsistencies in heat treatment lead to brittle tools that fail international "bend tests," causing bulk rejections.

## MOAT
A proprietary dataset of visual thermal markers mapped to the specific chemical composition of Pakistani surgical steel (410/420 grade).

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (8)**: Significantly better than the "naked eye" method currently used, though it requires specific lighting conditions near furnaces.
- **Adoption Criteria (8)**: High ROI; one batch rejection costs more than the tablet setup. Sialkot manufacturers are export-driven and willing to adopt tech that secures their foreign exchange.
- **Moat Confidence (9)**: Very high; local steel scrap mixes are unique. A global model trained on high-end German furnaces won't understand the visual markers of a Sialkot workshop.
