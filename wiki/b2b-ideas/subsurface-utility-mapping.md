# Subsurface Utility Mapping & Excavation Strike Prevention

![[problem_22_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_22_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: Fusing GPR and GIS data into an AR "X-ray" view of the ground to prevent striking buried utility lines during excavation.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Striking buried utility lines (power, gas, water, fiber) during excavation is a major safety hazard, causing service outages and expensive repairs. Currently, "locate" marks on the ground are often inaccurate or outdated.
By fusing Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) scans with historical GIS (Geographic Information System) maps, workers can gain "X-ray vision" into the earth before they dig.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Fuses real-time GPR sensor data with noisy historical GIS records to resolve conflicts and validate the exact depth and orientation of buried assets.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: 1:1 scale holographic overlay of pipes, cables, and conduits projected onto the excavation site through a headset or tablet, providing workers with visual certainty.

## Grounding
Massive infrastructure projects and urban maintenance in rapidly developing cities require frequent digging. Utility strike prevention reduces downtime, insurance premiums, and repair costs.

## MOAT
Localized, validated subsurface 3D data (as-built vs. as-mapped) and a soil-to-utility depth model that adjusts GPR signal interpretation based on local soil conditions.
