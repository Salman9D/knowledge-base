# Spatial Segregation Compliance for Hazardous Goods Warehousing

![[problem_27_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_27_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: AR-based compliance checking and virtual barriers to prevent the storage of incompatible hazardous chemicals in close proximity.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
In hazardous goods warehousing, storing reactive chemicals or incompatible materials (e.g., oxidizers near flammables) nearby is a critical safety violation that can lead to fire or explosion. Traditional compliance relies on manual checks of paper logs and physical labels, which are prone to human error.
A spatial AI system can automatically recognize chemical labels and enforce "virtual segregation" rules in real-time.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Uses computer vision to recognize Hazard Class labels (e.g., GHS icons) and continuously monitors the spatial proximity of stored goods against a database of regulatory segregation rules.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: Projects a red holographic barrier or "no-go zone" when a worker attempts to place a chemical drum in an incompatible area. High-risk near-misses trigger immediate visual and auditory warnings.

## Grounding
Chemical warehousing and industrial logistics centers face strict safety regulations. Preventing a single accidental reaction can save lives and millions in facility damage.

## MOAT
A comprehensive, digitised library of global and local regulatory chemical segregation rules, coupled with a dataset of "near-miss" compliance warnings that help facilities optimize their storage layouts.
