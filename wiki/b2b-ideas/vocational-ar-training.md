# Simulation Platform for Industrial Skill Training (Polytechnic Level)

![[problem_10_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_10_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: VR simulations and AR smart glasses provide hands-on practice, objective technique assessment, and automated competency certification for industrial trades.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Training quality of students being trained for industrial jobs (welding, fitting, machining, electrical) is abysmal because institutes lack equipment. An institute may have 2 lathes for 60 students. Students graduate without adequate hands-on practice. Industry spends 6 to 12 months retraining.
There can be VR simulation of workshop environments (lathe operation, arc welding, electrical panel wiring, CNC operation). If the student is wearing smart glasses, then a model can assess your technique such as hand steadiness in welding, tool approach angle in machining, wiring sequence in electrical work, etc. Haptic feedback integration for realistic tactile response would be the next step. There is also a possibility of automated competency certification with objective metrics (no more subjective assessor bias). It would require the development of industry-aligned curriculum modules (the industry itself can specify exact tasks they need trainees to master).

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Computer vision to assess physical technique (e.g., hand steadiness, tool approach angle), automated and objective competency certification based on industry metrics.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: VR environments simulating expensive workshop machinery (lathes, CNCs) for risk-free practice; AR overlays providing real-time visual corrections and sequence guidance during physical tasks.

## Grounding
Pakistan has a massive youth bulge and a critical need for skilled labor, yet vocational training institutes (like TEVTA) are critically underfunded and lack modern, working equipment. Students often share a single machine or practice with sub-standard materials. A VR/AR platform allows unlimited, safe "hands-on" practice without consumable waste, potentially revolutionizing the speed and quality of vocational training and making graduates immediately employable by local industries or for overseas export.

## MOAT
The moat is built on objective technique assessment data (which requires instrumented training sessions with expert validation) and competency progression analytics (which require longitudinal cohorts). If the platform achieves certification standard status, the moat becomes nearly insurmountable — the platform IS the standard. The industry partnership layer creates additional lock-in through co-invested curriculum modules.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (8)**: Addresses the critical lack of physical equipment and provides objective, standardizable grading.
- **Adoption Criteria (7)**: High impact, but adoption depends on government/NGO funding (TEVTA, NAVTTC) or B2B sales directly to large manufacturing firms for internal training.
- **Moat Confidence (8)**: Becoming the de facto "certification standard" through partnerships and accumulating technique data creates strong institutional lock-in.
