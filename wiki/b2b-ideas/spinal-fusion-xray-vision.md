# Intra-Operative X-Ray Vision for Complex Spinal Fusion Surgery

![[problem_21_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_21_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: Pedicle screw placement in complex spinal fusion surgery using an AR X-ray vision headset to prevent nerve damage.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Complex spinal fusion surgery involves the precise placement of pedicle screws. Even a minor deviation can sever a nerve root or damage the spinal cord, leading to paralysis. Surgeons currently rely on repeated intra-operative X-rays (fluoroscopy), which increases radiation exposure for both the patient and the surgical team. 
An AR-based X-ray vision headset allows surgeons to "see" the spine through skin and muscle, visualizing the internal bone structure in 3D without continuous radiation.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Real-time tracking of instrumented drills and pedicle screws against pre-operative 3D spinal topographies. It calculates the optimal trajectory and provides warnings if the drill deviates from the safe path.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: Holographic projection of the patient's spine and planned screw trajectories directly onto the surgical site, providing the surgeon with "X-ray vision."

## Grounding
Specialized orthopedic and neurosurgery centers handle a high volume of spinal cases. Reducing surgical time and improving accuracy directly translates to better patient outcomes and lower litigation risks. While the hardware cost is high, the reduction in fluoroscopy time and post-operative complications provides a strong ROI.

## MOAT
Instrumented drill trajectory data, unique spinal topographies across diverse patient demographics, and a proprietary database of planning-to-placement variance used to refine the AI's predictive safety margins.
