# Warehouse Slotting Optimization & Picking in Cold Chain Logistics

![[problem_15_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_15_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: Optimizing warehouse slotting and picking in extreme cold chain environments (-25C to +8C) using spatial-thermal models and AR guidance.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Cold chain logistics (frozen food, pharmaceuticals) operates in extreme environments where worker efficiency is limited by temperature and equipment must be ruggedized. Managing "slotting" (where items are placed) is critical to minimize the time workers spend in sub-zero zones and to ensure thermal stability for sensitive products.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **Spatial-Thermal Modeling**: Creates a 3D model of the warehouse that includes real-time thermal gradients, identifying the most stable zones for specific products.
- **AR Picking Guidance**: Provides workers with visual paths and slotting instructions through AR glasses (optimized for extreme cold), reducing "search time" in hazardous temperatures.
- **Dynamic Slotting Optimization**: AI re-slots inventory based on spatial proximity to loading docks and thermal requirements to minimize energy use and transit time.

## Grounding
As Pakistan's pharmaceutical and processed food exports grow, the need for reliable cold chain infrastructure is paramount. Inefficient cold storage leads to high energy costs and product wastage (spoilage). A spatial solution that optimizes movement and storage in these energy-intensive environments directly impacts the bottom line and export quality.

## MOAT
The moat is based on specialized spatial-thermal models and operational data from extreme environments. AR hardware and software that can function reliably at -25C while providing high-accuracy spatial guidance is a significant technical hurdle. Proprietary data on thermal-to-operational efficiency correlations in local warehouses provides a compounding advantage.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (8)**: Directly addresses the high-cost, high-risk nature of cold chain operations with spatial optimization.
- **Adoption Criteria (8)**: High ROI for pharma and food exporters who face strict international quality standards and high energy costs.
- **Moat Confidence (8)**: Strong defensibility through specialized hardware/software requirements and proprietary thermal data.
