# Aluminum Cookware Casting Defect Scanner

**Summary**: Tablet-based visual scan of cast aluminum pots to detect air bubbles or surface pits before polishing.

**Sources**: [[pakistan-b2b-ideas-20]]

---

## Description
Gujranwala is the hub for aluminum cookware. "Sand casting" often leaves tiny air bubbles that only appear after expensive polishing. A vision scanner detects these pits on the raw cast, allowing for immediate re-melting.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Acts as a real-time "expert eye," using computer vision to identify tiny air bubbles and surface pits on raw aluminum casts before they undergo expensive polishing.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: Provides tablet-first visual guidance, highlighting each detected defect directly on the screen's view of the pot, allowing workers to quickly decide which pieces to re-melt.

## Grounding
Polishing is the most labor-intensive and expensive part of cookware production. Detecting defects early saves hours of wasted labor.

## MOAT
Visual markers of sand-casting defects in Pakistani recycled aluminum.

## Scoring Debate
- **Solution Quality (8)**: Direct labor-saving tool.
- **Adoption Criteria (7)**: Gujranwala makers are cost-sensitive but understand labor efficiency.
- **Moat Confidence (9)**: Niche; recycled aluminum has unique surface textures.
