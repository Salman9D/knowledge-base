# Cost Intelligence for Construction

![[problem_28_img1.jpeg]]
![[problem_28_img2.jpeg]]

**Summary**: Using spatial site scans and AI to automatically quantify material installation and track project financials in real-time.

**Sources**: [[B2B Problem Statements (AR_VR_MR).pdf]]

---

## Description
Tracking progress and costs on construction sites is notoriously manual, slow, and prone to disputes. Project managers often rely on rough estimates of "percentage complete" which leads to budget overruns and cash flow issues.
Spatial data captures the exact state of the site, allowing for automated "Cost Intelligence" by linking physical installation to financial accounting.

## Spatial AI Solution
- **AI Spatial Assistant**: Automatically identifies and quantifies installed materials (e.g., number of bricks laid, linear meters of conduit installed) from periodic 3D site scans. It compares this against the Bill of Quantities (BOQ) to update project financials.
- **AR/VR Overlay**: Displays "Budget vs. Actual" progress overlays directly on the construction site for project managers, highlighting areas that are over budget or behind schedule.

## Grounding
Construction cost overruns are a global problem. Providing developers and contractors with real-time visibility into material usage and costs reduces waste, prevents over-billing, and improves financial predictability.

## MOAT
A proprietary database linking historical material-to-labor-to-cost data across diverse project types, combined with regional pricing intelligence that allows for more accurate predictive budgeting.
