## Meeting Purpose

Review the first sprint of XR market research and realign product direction. The session resulted in one major strategic decision: abandon the B2C calorie-tracking POC and pivot to B2B enterprise verticals.

---

## Key Decisions

### 1. B2C → B2B Pivot (decision owner: Moiz)

The initial POC concept — an AR diet/calorie assistant targeting consumers — was challenged and rejected.

**Why it was rejected**:
- Nutrition/calorie tracking is commoditized; AR offers only incremental value
- B2C success depends on slow consumer adoption of AR glasses (small near-term TAM)
- The general-purpose B2C assistant space will be won by Google/Apple/Meta

**New direction**: Target B2B enterprise verticals where a spatial AI assistant solves a critical, high-frequency workflow problem with clear ROI.

### 2. Target Vertical Criteria

Any vertical considered for a POC must satisfy all three:
- **High-frequency glasses usage** — the task naturally recurs with hands occupied
- **Clear ROI** — measurable error reduction, task time savings, or training hours reduction
- **Data moat potential + accessible pilot** — a local enterprise partner who will grant exclusive data access

### 3. Named Target Verticals

| Vertical | Core Problem |
|----------|-------------|
| **Industrial training / factory floors** | Complex procedural tasks, safety protocols, skill transfer |
| **Data center operations** | Complex wiring, rack configurations, fault diagnosis |
| **Labs (chemical/pharma)** | Composition verification, error prevention, compliance |
| **Construction** | Real-time plan validation, site inspection, defect detection |

### 4. Data Moat as the Core Defensibility Thesis

Technology moats are eroding under AI commoditization — any model can be replicated. The sustainable moat is **proprietary task/scene data** that competitors cannot acquire.

**Mechanism**:
1. Partner with an enterprise at no cost (build for free to gain access)
2. Capture exclusive proprietary data from real workflows
3. Train a specialized AI model on that data
4. Compound the moat through enterprise rollouts (network effects)

**Outcome**: A defensible asset that makes the business an attractive acquisition target.

### 5. Strategic Constraints Confirmed

- **AR-first**: Not VR/MR; content/apps segment has >40% CAGR
- **No hardware/OS plays**: Build cross-platform on top of Horizon OS, visionOS, Android XR
- **On-device-first architecture**: For privacy/latency compliance (GDPR/EOI)
- **Track glasses roadmaps**: Meta Ray-Ban, Samsung/Meta collaborations, Google efforts

## Market Data Confirmed in Session

- $76B (2024) → $462B (2030), 24–35% CAGR
- AR largest tech segment at ~$40B (2024)
- Content/apps: >40% CAGR — highest growth sub-segment
- Hardware shift: bulky headsets → lightweight smart glasses post-2025
- North America: 38% share; Asia-Pacific: 32% share, highest CAGR
- Enterprise technology adoption historically precedes consumer
