# B2B Evaluation Criteria (Global)

**Summary**: Global framework and constraints used to score and select viable B2B AR/VR/XR spatial AI use cases. Targets international scalability across multiple markets.

**Sources**: [[Spatial AI Synch - May 07.txt]], Spatial_AI_Venture_Evaluation_Framework.docx

## Evaluation Scoping

The team evaluates potential B2B ideas using a Quality/Adoption/Moat scoring system (1-10 each) to filter for the most viable projects with global market potential.

---

## Key Criteria & Constraints

### 1. Strategic Viability
- **Defensive MOAT**: Does the solution build a barrier through proprietary data, deep industry integration, or unique workflow intelligence?
- **Big Tech Avoidance**: Is the use case specific enough that Google/Meta/Apple are unlikely to build it as a platform feature?

### 2. Global Market Penetration
- **International Scalability**: Can the solution work across multiple countries with limited localization?
  - *Evaluate*: Language dependency, regulatory differences, infrastructure requirements, hardware dependency
- **Industry Adoption Readiness**: Will enterprises globally allow data collection, facility testing, and workflow integration?
  - *Easier*: Warehousing, Manufacturing, Logistics, Training
  - *Harder*: Hospitals, Defense, Government

### 3. Technical & Operational Constraints
- **MVP Feasibility (2-3 Months)**: Can a compelling prototype be built with current team? Can existing AI APIs/models handle core functionality?
- **Hardware Realism**: Is the required hardware practical today? (Mobile AR, Tablets preferred over Custom smart glasses)
- **AI/Spatial Complexity**: Is the technical challenge achievable? (Object detection overlays preferred over persistent world understanding)

### 4. Commercial Viability
- **ROI Clarity**: Can you clearly prove cost reduction, faster workflows, higher throughput, lower error rates, or increased revenue?
- **Recurring Revenue Potential**: Can the business become SaaS, device + software subscription, per-seat enterprise licensing, or usage-based AI infrastructure?
- **Market Urgency**: Is the problem painful enough that companies globally seek solutions? (Safety risk, high operational cost, skilled labor shortages, expensive downtime)

---

## Simplified Scoring Framework

| Criteria | Definition | Score Range |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| **Quality** | How strong, useful, technically feasible, and valuable the solution is | 1-10 |
| **Adoption** | Likelihood of enterprise/customer adoption and scalability | 1-10 |
| **Moat** | Strength of defensibility through data, workflows, integrations, or proprietary systems | 1-10 |

---

## Next Steps

1. List all ideas in a spreadsheet or database
2. Score each idea on Quality, Adoption, and Moat
3. Add notes for technical risk, market timing, and strategic observations
4. Prioritize ideas with strong scores across all three dimensions
5. Select top candidates for pilot validation and deeper market research
