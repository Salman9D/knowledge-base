## Evaluation Scoping

The team plans to evaluate potential B2B ideas using an LLM-based scoring system to filter for the most viable 3-5 projects (source: Spatial AI Synch - May 07.txt).

## Key Criteria & Constraints

### 1. Strategic Viability
- **Defensive MOAT**: Does the solution build a barrier through proprietary data or deep industry integration?
- **Big Tech Avoidance**: Is the use case specific enough that Google/Meta/Apple are unlikely to build it as a platform feature?

### 2. Market Penetration
- **Industry Willingness**: Will the industry (e.g., medical, textile) allow access to their data and facilities for testing?
- **Local Testing Potential**: Can the idea be piloted in the local (Pakistani) market first? (source: Spatial AI Synch - May 07.txt).

### 3. Technical & Operational Constraints
- **Technical Competence**: Does the team have the skills to build the solution within 2-3 months?
- **Gadget Availability**: Is the required hardware (tablets, headsets, or smart glasses) feasible for the environment?
- **Sensitivities**: Are there high-risk factors (e.g., heart surgery vs. limb rehab) that might limit adoption?

### 4. Commercial Viability
- **Labor Intensity Reduction**: Does the solution significantly reduce the need for in-person experts (e.g., therapists)?
- **Fast-Paced Utility**: Does it provide clear value in high-stakes or time-sensitive environments?

## Next Steps

The team is consolidating a list of 15-20 ideas to be scored against these criteria in upcoming meetings (source: Spatial AI Synch - May 07.txt).
