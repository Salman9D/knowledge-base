## 2026 Consolidated Market Projections

The AR and VR markets are diverging. AR is becoming a mass-market utility (Retail, Navigation, Guidance), while VR is concentrating in high-immersion skills and entertainment.

### Broad AR Market (Broad Scope)
- **2025 Market Size**: **$120.21 billion**
- **2033 Projection**: **$1,050.56 billion**
- **CAGR**: **29.7%**

### Broad VR Market (Narrower Scope)
- **2025 Market Size**: **$20.83 billion**
- **2032 Projection**: **$123.06 billion**
- **CAGR**: **28.9%**

## Consumer Demand & Interest Signals (Deloitte/S&P 2025)

The appetite for AR/VR is driven by practical utility rather than "abstract immersion."

- **Interest Level**: **48.2%** of nonowners are interested in buying smart glasses.
- **Glasses vs. VR**: Interest in smart glasses is **7 percentage points higher** than in VR headsets.
- **Purchase Drivers**: Curiosity (41%), **Usefulness (39%)**, Fun (37%).
- **Purchase Barriers**: **Lack of clear need (50%)**, Cost (41%).
- **Favorable Impression**: 56% of consumers have a favorable view of smart eyewear when AI/Translation/Audio features are explained.

## Strategic Trust & Safety Signals
- **Privacy Concerns**: **70%** of consumers worry about data privacy in digital services.
- **Misuse Anxiety**: **82%** of consumers worry that GenAI could be misused.
- **Backlash Risk**: Smart glasses adoption is triggering social backlash around covert recording and consent.
