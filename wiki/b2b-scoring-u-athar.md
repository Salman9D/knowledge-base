## Four-Property Filter Key

**Buildable**: Can an MVP be built with existing CV/Spatial AI tools in under 12 weeks?
**Testable locally**: Can it be piloted in Pakistan without a special facility or clearance?
**Big-Tech-safe**: Niche enough that Google/Meta/Apple will not absorb it as a platform feature?
**Data flywheel**: Does deployment generate proprietary training data that compounds over time?

Rating: **Strong / Partial / Weak / None**

## Detailed Entries (alphabetical by filename)

### 1. aircraft-engine-inspection
- **Quality 9**: Borescope CV + 3D blade mapping maps well to fine-tuned defect detection models; SafetyNet-grade accuracy (>97%) is achievable with turbine-specific datasets, but labeled data acquisition requires MRO partnerships.
- **Adoption 7**: High-value buyers (PIA, private MRO) exist, but FAA/EASA/CAA regulatory sign-off adds 6–18 months of validation overhead before live deployment.
- **Moat 9**: Proprietary turbine defect image bank + longitudinal crack-growth mapping is essentially unreplicable without MRO data access; switching cost is extremely high once embedded in maintenance workflows.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Partial (need MRO partner) | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: Regulatory timeline extends payback; requires specialized borescope hardware integration.
- **Recommendation**: High priority — pursue MRO partnership first; treat regulatory approval as a 12-month parallel workstream.
- Key Risk: If CAA mandates OEM-only inspection software, third-party tools may be legally blocked.

### 2. auto-parts-verification
- **Quality 8**: Tablet-based step-by-step assembly verification uses off-the-shelf object detection (YOLOv8) with a custom part catalog; fine-tuning on local SKUs is a 4–6 week effort.
- **Adoption 7**: Tier-2 auto vendors (Suzuki, Honda supply chain) are cost-sensitive; ROI is clear but change management with factory floor workers adds 2–3 months of onboarding.
- **Moat 9**: Assembly failure patterns + defect rates per local part SKU build a dataset that global competitors cannot access without factory floor presence.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: None critical.
- **Recommendation**: Strong candidate — clear problem, testable in Karachi/Lahore industrial zones, and data moat builds quickly.
- Key Risk: Auto OEMs may mandate their own QC software as a vendor requirement, blocking third-party tools.

### 3. avionics-wiring-assembly
- **Quality 9**: CAD-to-reality mapping + pin-level verification leverages spatial registration models (FoundationPose, ARCore anchors); wire-routing CV is technically complex but achievable with depth cameras.
- **Adoption 8**: Aerospace/satellite manufacturing clients (SUPARCO) have high tolerance for integration cost given the catastrophic cost of a wiring error; headset deployment is justified.
- **Moat 8**: CAD integration telemetry + temporal error-correction dataset creates per-client lock-in; standard spatial wiring tools exist globally but none trained on local aerospace variants.
- Buildable: Partial (depth+RGB required, CAD pipeline complex) | Testable locally: Partial (SUPARCO clearances needed) | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: Defense/space clearance may be required; very small addressable market in Pakistan currently.
- **Recommendation**: Medium priority — technically impressive but market size is limited locally; revisit when SUPARCO partnership is secured.
- Key Risk: Small number of local clients means data flywheel is slow to build.

### 4. brick-kiln-monitoring
- **Quality 7**: Brick counting and smoke-color emission estimation are achievable with YOLO + color histogram analysis; outdoor lighting variability and smoke occlusion are significant CV challenges.
- **Adoption 5**: Brick kiln operators are largely informal sector; they resist regulatory tools, and consistent power for tablet charging is unreliable in kiln environments.
- **Moat 7**: Compliance and output dataset for the informal construction sector has value to government/NGOs but limited commercial defensibility.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Partial (informal sector buy-in needed) | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Partial
- Disqualifiers: Business model unclear — informal sector won't pay; NGO/government grant dependency.
- **Recommendation**: Deprioritize — low adoption ceiling without regulatory mandate; revisit only if SEPA or Punjab Environment Department becomes a paying customer.
- Key Risk: Customers are non-paying or enforcement-dependent; no clear B2B revenue path.

### 5. brownfield-digital-twins
- **Quality 8**: LiDAR + photogrammetry to auto-generate plant digital twins is a mature pipeline (Matterport-style); the AI challenge is semantic feature recognition (pipes, valves) in cluttered industrial scenes — achievable but requires custom training.
- **Adoption 8**: Aging textile/chemical plants (Faisalabad, Hub) have clear retrofit planning needs; LiDAR scanner rental lowers CapEx barrier for initial pilots.
- **Moat 8**: Facility-specific digital twins + years of integrated maintenance logs create a "once twinned, always twinned" customer stickiness; competitors must rebuild from scratch for each facility.
- Buildable: Partial (LiDAR hardware + semantic segmentation pipeline) | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: Initial scan cost (LiDAR hardware rental ~$500–2K/day) is a barrier for smaller factories.
- **Recommendation**: High priority — large industrial base in Pakistan, clear ROI, and facility-specific data creates durable moat.
- Key Risk: 3D scanning pipelines for brownfield plants are messy; model training on legacy Pakistani factory layouts requires significant labeling effort.

- Buildable: Partial (RF modeling is domain-specific, not just CV) | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong

- **Recommendation**: High priority — strong telco buyer with clear ROI, but hire or partner with RF engineers before building.
- Key Risk: Telco network data is commercially sensitive; carriers may refuse to share signal performance data needed to train the RF prediction model.

### 7. cement-kiln-inspection

- **Adoption 6**: Cement companies (Lucky, Bestway) have budgets but shutdown windows are infrequent and short, leaving minimal time for onboarding a new system; AR glasses in kiln environments face thermal tolerance issues.
- **Moat 8**: Kiln wear models specific to local raw material composition (Balochistan limestone vs. imported clinker) are genuinely niche and defensible.
- Buildable: Partial (requires thermal camera + custom wear model) | Testable locally: Partial (shutdown-only access) | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Partial
- Disqualifiers: Infrequent shutdown windows limit data collection speed; device thermal tolerance may require ruggedized hardware.
- **Recommendation**: Medium priority — high-value problem but slow data collection cycle; consider as a complementary offering alongside brownfield digital twins.
- Key Risk: If shutdown windows are only 2–4 per year per plant, training data accumulation will take 2–3 years.

- **Moat 9**: "What goes wrong in Pakistani construction" defect dataset is uniquely local — rebar spacing norms, concrete pour practices, and local building codes differ from global datasets; this becomes a highly defensible local intelligence asset.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong

- **Recommendation**: TOP PRIORITY — highest Quality score (10), testable immediately with any large Karachi/Lahore construction project, and the data moat is locally specific.

- **Adoption 9**: FMCG merchandisers already conduct store visits; existing smartphones require no hardware change; FMCG companies (Coca-Cola, Nestle) lose 20–30% of marketing spend to "display leakage."
- **Moat 7**: Visual dataset of thousands of Pakistani Kiryana and general store environments is a significant data effort; real moat is the distribution-brand partnership lock-in.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Partial (Nielsen, Field Star operate in this space) | Data flywheel: Partial
- Disqualifiers: Competing with established field-force analytics platforms (Nielsen, Nuvoco); differentiation on spatial AI + photo evidence quality.
- **Recommendation**: High priority for quick revenue — FMCG brands are immediately reachable and have clear budget; approach Coca-Cola Pakistan or Nestle Pakistan for a pilot.
- Key Risk: FMCG companies may mandate use of their global retail analytics platform (Nielsen Retail Measurement) rather than a local solution.

### 55. scrap-metal-sorting
- **Quality 8**: Vision-based metal type classification (copper, aluminum, steel, brass) using texture and oxidation pattern analysis is achievable with custom training on Gadani/Port Qasim scrap samples.
- **Adoption 7**: Gadani ship-breaking is a rough outdoor environment; financially motivated scrap yard operators understand the value of catching 10kg of copper that would otherwise be missed.
- **Moat 9**: Visual data on aged/corroded marine-grade metals from Gadani's specific scrap mix is genuinely unique; global metal sorting models are trained on clean industrial scrap, not marine-weathered metal.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: Outdoor, dusty, variable-lighting Gadani environment requires ruggedized, weatherproof camera setup.
- **Recommendation**: High priority — Gadani is a major industrial cluster; data moat is highly localized; deploy as a fixed-camera rig at sorting stations rather than handheld for durability.
- Key Risk: Chemical contamination and salt corrosion on marine metals makes visual classification harder than clean industrial scrap; extensive field data collection needed.

### 56. seed-purity-vision
- **Quality 8**: Seed purity analysis (weed seed detection + impurity counting) on a backlit tablet tray is a well-defined top-down CV task; achieving high recall on weed seeds that visually resemble target seeds is the key challenge.
- **Adoption 7**: 1,000+ local seed companies in Punjab are the target, but many are small-scale; most viable approach is through PVMC (Pakistan Variety Maintenance Committee) or large seed companies (Pioneer, Syngenta Pakistan).
- **Moat 9**: Visual catalog of local weed seeds + Pakistani seed varieties is a nationally significant dataset; unique weed profiles in Pakistan (Phalaris minor, wild oat) are not in global seed databases.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: Target market of small seed companies has low willingness/ability to pay; monetize through large seed companies or government agricultural extension programs.
- **Recommendation**: High priority as an agricultural sector play — pair with crop-disease-scouting for a comprehensive agricultural quality platform; approach Syngenta Pakistan or PVMC for pilot partnership.
- Key Risk: Class imbalance (weed seeds are rare in samples) makes achieving high recall difficult; model needs extensive augmentation and hard-negative mining.

### 57. semiconductor-euv-maintenance
- **Quality 9**: XR-guided maintenance for EUV lithography systems (live digital twin projection + spatial telemetry + guided troubleshooting) is technically the most sophisticated idea in this set; nanometer-level spatial registration is an extraordinary engineering challenge.
- **Adoption 6**: Extremely limited facilities globally (~500 EUV machines worldwide, none in Pakistan); cleanroom environment + ASML security restrictions make access extremely difficult for third-party software vendors.
- **Moat 9**: Restricted telemetry access + spatial maintenance logs + highly secure fab environment = enormous barriers to entry; once deployed, switching is essentially impossible.
- Buildable: Weak (nanometer-level spatial registration is beyond current consumer-grade AR hardware accuracy) | Testable locally: None | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: No EUV facilities in Pakistan or anywhere in South Asia; requires ASML partnership to access machine telemetry; not relevant to Pakistan market.
- **Recommendation**: Deprioritize for Pakistan market — internationally significant but requires direct ASML partnership and global sales capability; not a near-term priority.
- Key Risk: ASML has exclusive control over EUV telemetry and machine access; no startup can deploy in EUV cleanrooms without ASML's approval.

### 58. solar-health-monitor
- **Quality 8**: Visual detection of dust buildup, micro-cracks, and thermal hot-spots on rooftop solar panels via tablet camera is technically achievable; thermal imaging requires a FLIR-style thermal attachment for hot-spot detection (non-visual hot-spots).
- **Adoption 8**: Pakistan's solar market is booming (massive rooftop solar surge since 2023–2024); solar maintenance companies are a rapidly growing B2B sector; tablet deployment fits existing technician workflows.
- **Moat 9**: Dataset of solar panel performance degradation patterns specific to Pakistani dust and pollution profiles is highly localized; "calima"-style dust from the Thar desert has unique particle characteristics that affect panel degradation differently than European or East Asian markets.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: Thermal hot-spot detection (beyond visual dust inspection) requires a ~$300–500 thermal camera attachment; consider visual-only MVP first.
- **Recommendation**: TOP PRIORITY — Pakistan's solar boom creates an immediately addressable and rapidly growing market; data moat is locally unique; quick MVP path with visual-only inspection.
- Key Risk: Without thermal imaging, hot-spot detection is limited to visible surface defects; thermal camera attachment adds cost but is necessary for complete performance diagnosis.

### 59. spinal-fusion-xray-vision
- **Quality 9**: AR "X-ray vision" for pedicle screw placement + real-time drill trajectory tracking against pre-operative 3D spinal models is technically achievable; the challenge is real-time patient registration during surgery (patient moves).
- **Adoption 6**: Specialized orthopedic/neurosurgery centers handle high spinal fusion volumes; barriers are DRAP regulatory approval and surgeon adoption of intra-operative AR hardware.
- **Moat 9**: Instrumented drill trajectory data + diverse spinal topographies across Pakistani patient demographics + planning-to-placement variance database = a surgical navigation AI moat that rivals the best in the world.
- Buildable: Partial (real-time patient registration during surgery; sterile hardware) | Testable locally: Partial (orthopedic center partnership needed) | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: DRAP regulatory approval; clinical validation study required; intra-operative patient motion registration is technically demanding.
- **Recommendation**: High priority as a long-term clinical R&D investment — pursue a major orthopedic center (Aga Khan Ortho, CMH) as clinical research partner; 2–3 year development timeline.
- Key Risk: Intra-operative patient registration (accounting for patient breathing and movement) is the hardest spatial alignment problem in this surgical domain; requires specialized IR or optical tracking hardware.

### 60. sports-apparel-alignment
- **Quality 9**: 2mm-precision fabric placement verification before heat press via fixed CV rig is technically achievable; digital ghost overlay for alignment is a straightforward spatial registration task.
- **Adoption 7**: Requires a fixed camera rig above the heat press station; slightly more complex than handheld but the ROI from eliminating misalignment-caused "Seconds" is substantial.
- **Moat 9**: Dataset of seam and pattern recognition for specific sports silhouettes (FIFA, NBA, NFL standard cuts) manufactured in Sialkot is highly specialized; global sportswear brands cannot easily share this pattern data.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: None.
- **Recommendation**: TOP PRIORITY — Sialkot is Pakistan's premier manufacturing export hub; data moat is export-contract-specific; pairs with glove-seam-inspector and sports-goods-stitching for a comprehensive Sialkot manufacturing QC platform.
- Key Risk: Export contract pattern specifications change seasonally; model retraining cadence must keep up with new season garment patterns.

### 61. sports-coaching-ar-assistant
- **Quality 8**: Markerless multi-person pose estimation (MMPose, MediaPipe) is technically mature; simultaneous tracking of all athletes on a field + per-player AR overlay in a fixed-camera tablet MVP is achievable in 6–8 weeks. Real-time smart glasses mode is a feasible second phase. Sport-specific biomechanics models (cricket bowling run-up, hockey deceleration mechanics) add meaningful research novelty.
- **Adoption 8**: Sports academies and national federations (PCB, PHF) are accessible pilot partners with genuine need; tablet + fixed-camera setup is affordable and requires no athlete wearables; the coach's existing fieldside workflow naturally accommodates the tool. Smart glasses hardware is a cost friction point but tablet-first mode eliminates it for the MVP.
- **Moat 9**: Longitudinal per-player biomechanics baselines — running mechanics, joint angles at peak speed, fatigue-signature gait drift, sport-specific injury precursors — compound with every session. Sport-specific models trained on Pakistani cricket and hockey player populations are not replicable by a global competitor without years of local training data access. Coach workflow integration (drill schemas, session summaries, per-athlete flag history) adds behavioral switching costs on top of the data flywheel.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: None. Smart glasses hardware cost is a friction point for grassroots academies but tablet-first mode fully resolves it for the MVP.
- **Recommendation**: High priority — clear spatial AI fit (multi-person real-time biomechanics is a genuinely novel AR application), accessible pilot pathway through PCB U-19 academy or PHF training camp, and a data moat that builds with every session. Positions Imagination AI / 9D as the "sports science democratizer" for academies that cannot afford GPS vest systems or VICON motion capture labs.
- Key Risk: GPS vest analytics vendors (Catapult Sports, STATSports) could add a markerless vision layer to their existing platforms; differentiate on the AR overlay experience, zero-wearable athlete UX, and the depth of the per-player longitudinal baseline.
- Suggested first step: Build a fixed-camera tablet prototype; run one session with PCB or PHF; measure pace tracking accuracy, coach time-to-feedback reduction, and athlete technique error detection rate.

### 62. sports-goods-stitching

- **Moat 8**: Assembly workflow data for specific sporting goods models (hand-stitched vs. thermo-molded) is defensible; model-specific stitch patterns differ between FIFA match balls, training balls, and cricket equipment.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: 3D curved surface inspection (football panels) is harder than flat seam inspection; may need structured light or depth camera for accurate stitch counting on curved surfaces.
- **Recommendation**: High priority as part of the Sialkot QC platform — bundle with glove-seam-inspector and sports-apparel-alignment for combined offering.
- Key Risk: 3D curved surface inspection on hand-stitched footballs requires structured lighting or depth sensing; standard tablet camera may be insufficient for accurate curved-surface stitch counting.

### 63. subsurface-utility-mapping

- Key Risk: Existing GIS utility maps in Pakistan are notoriously inaccurate; the system will frequently encounter "as-built" vs. "as-mapped" discrepancies that challenge the spatial fusion model.

### 66. telecom-tower-maintenance

- **Moat 9**: Telecom asset fault history per carrier infrastructure (carrier-specific component aging patterns, failure frequencies by region) is a deeply local and proprietary dataset.

### 67. termite-detection-vision

### 68. textile-quality-control

- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Partial (global textile CV vendors exist) | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: Global textile inspection CV vendors (EG&G, AMS Technologies) already operate; differentiate on local cotton specificity and price.
- **Recommendation**: TOP PRIORITY — Pakistan's largest export sector with immediate commercial opportunity; approach Nishat Mills or Interloop for a pilot; revenue through per-meter inspection licensing or monthly SLA.
- Key Risk: Global textile inspection vendors are well-established; local solution must demonstrate measurably better defect detection on Pakistani cotton vs. global models to win contracts.

### 69. truck-loading-optimizer
- **Quality 8**: 3D cargo space measurement + parcel dimension estimation + optimal stacking suggestion via tablet AR is technically achievable; 3D bin packing algorithms are well-established; the CV challenge is reliable cargo space measurement in dusty truck environments.

- **Moat 7**: Dataset of irregular Pakistani parcel types + local truck dimension profiles (Hino, Mazda, Isuzu configurations) is moderately defensible but replicable.
- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Partial (logistics optimization algorithms are available; Amazon has patented versions) | Data flywheel: Partial
- Disqualifiers: 3D bin packing is computationally well-understood; moat is primarily the local truck-type + parcel-type dataset, not the algorithm.
- **Recommendation**: Medium priority — viable and deployable but moderate moat; consider as a module within last-mile-condition-log + warehouse-vision-picker for a complete logistics platform.
- Key Risk: Amazon, Alibaba, and large logistics platforms have highly optimized packing algorithms; the local differentiation (Pakistani truck/parcel types) must be clearly demonstrated.

### 70. underground-mine-safety
- **Quality 9**: SLAM-based digital twin generation of underground mines + IoT gas/temperature sensor integration + predictive hazard modeling is technically ambitious; GPS-denied SLAM in confined underground spaces is achievable with LiDAR-based systems.
- **Adoption 7**: Large mining companies (PMDC coal, Reko Diq, Duddar zinc) have high ROI for safety; hardware costs and ruggedization requirements are significant deployment barriers.
- **Moat 9**: Longitudinal hazard data from active mine excavations (pre- and post-incident spatial data) is an extraordinarily rare and defensible dataset; no generic AI model can be trained on this data without physical mine access.
- Buildable: Partial (GPS-denied SLAM + ruggedized hardware; 3–6 month MVP) | Testable locally: Partial (mining company partnership needed) | Big-Tech-safe: Strong | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: High hardware cost (ruggedized SLAM scanners); requires mining company partnership for access.
- **Recommendation**: High priority as a strategic long-term play — Pakistan's mineral wealth and mining safety record make this nationally significant; pursue PMDC or Barrick Gold (Reko Diq) as a pilot partner.
- Key Risk: Mine environments are GPS-denied, dusty, and dynamically changing; SLAM drift in long underground tunnels is a known accuracy challenge that requires careful sensor calibration.

### 71. urban-planning-smart-cities
- **Quality 8**: City-scale digital twin integrating GIS + IoT + architectural models + scenario simulation (traffic, energy, heat islands) is technically ambitious but achievable with existing smart city platforms (CityEngine, Esri ArcGIS).

- **Moat 8**: Proprietary geo-spatial urban datasets + infrastructure behavior models + institutional lock-in as the central municipal data repository creates a durable moat; once a city's planning workflows depend on the platform, switching is extremely costly.
- Buildable: Partial (city-scale data integration; 6–12 month MVP) | Testable locally: Partial (government cooperation required) | Big-Tech-safe: Partial (Esri, IBM Intelligent Operations Center operate here) | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: Government procurement in Pakistan is slow and politically driven; global smart city platforms (Esri, IBM, Siemens) are direct competitors.
- **Recommendation**: Medium priority — high strategic value but long sales cycle; approach Lahore's Smart City initiative or Islamabad Capital Territory Administration for a pilot; position as the "Pakistan-specific" smart city solution.
- Key Risk: Smart city contracts in Pakistan are often captured by politically connected large IT integrators; independent startup entry is difficult without government connections.

### 72. vocational-ar-training

- Buildable: Strong | Testable locally: Strong | Big-Tech-safe: Partial (global EdTech VR companies are adjacent) | Data flywheel: Strong
- Disqualifiers: Government adoption cycles are slow; commercial viability depends on either TEVTA/NAVTTC partnership or direct industry sales.
- **Recommendation**: High priority as a social impact + commercial play — Pakistan's youth bulge and vocational skills gap are pressing national problems; approach large manufacturers (Millat Tractors, Atlas Honda) for in-house training platform first, then scale to TEVTA.
- Key Risk: Government vocational training institutions (TEVTA) have 3–5 year procurement cycles and often prefer established local vendors; private manufacturing sector sales are faster but smaller scale.

### 73. warehouse-vision-picker
