## Hardware Market Overview

Hardware remains the largest revenue segment of the AR market, accounting for **more than 59% of AR revenue in 2025**. However, the form factor is undergoing a radical transition.

### The Great Form Factor Shift (2025–2026)
The strongest signal from the 2025/2026 market data is the divergence between smart glasses and traditional headsets:
- **Smart Glasses Growth**: Shipments expanded **44.4% in 2025**, driven by AI-enabled, lightweight eyewear (source: IDC).
- **Headset Decline**: Traditional VR and mixed-reality (MR) headset shipments declined in 2025 as consumers shifted toward always-on wearable glasses.
- **Shipment Velocity**: IDC expects XR shipments to grow **33.5% in 2026**, led almost entirely by the glasses segment.

## Device Categories

### Smart Glasses (AI-First Wearables)
This is currently the fastest-growing display segment and the most important near-term hardware signal.
- **Meta Ray-Ban Smart Glasses**: $299. The "Trojan Horse" of AR. Sells on style/audio while habituating users to camera-based AI.
- **Meta Ray-Ban Blayzer + Scriber Optics**: $499. Prescription-optimized for all-day wear.
- **Snap Specs (Upcoming 2026)**: Snapdragon XR-based lightweight consumer AR glasses.
- **Samsung Galaxy Glasses (Upcoming H2 2026)**: Android XR glasses with confirmed displays.
- **VITURE Beast XR**: Dual Sony micro-OLED (1080p, 1250 nits) with electrochromic lenses for AR/VR switching.

### Head-Mounted Displays (HMDs)
Traditional headsets are becoming increasingly specialized for high-immersion "full-head" experiences (training, simulation, gaming).
- **Consumer Standalone**: Meta Quest 3 ($499) is the volume leader, though Quest sales saw a -16% decline YoY as focus shifted to glasses.
- **Premium/Prosumer**: 
    - **Apple Vision Pro**: $3,499. The gold standard for passthrough and spatial fidelity.
    - **Samsung Galaxy XR**: $1,799. The first major device to enter the premium gap between consumer and enterprise pricing.
- **Enterprise AR**: Magic Leap 2 and HoloLens 2 continue to dominate industrial and medical segments.

## Spatial Mapping Hardware Technologies

For a Spatial AI Assistant to work, hardware must support environment perception:

### 1. LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging)
- **Best for**: Accuracy, large spaces, low-light.
- **Used in**: Apple Vision Pro, iPhone Pro models.

### 2. Stereo Camera + SLAM
- **Best for**: Scalability, cost-effectiveness.
- **Used in**: Meta Quest 3, HoloLens 2.
- **Signal**: The combination of stereo RGB cameras and on-device SLAM is the dominant architecture for the "always-on" AI vision required by assistants.

## Hardware Competitive Dynamics

- **Meta Dominance**: Meta held **72.2% of the global XR market in 2025**. Their lead is driven by vertical integration and aggressive pricing.
- **Qualcomm vs. Apple**: The Snapdragon XR2 Gen 2 chip defines the performance ceiling for the Android XR ecosystem, while Apple's M5+R1 custom silicon maintains a significant lead in sensor-to-photon latency.
- **Hardware Casualties**: **Lynx** (liquidation, Mar 2026) serves as a warning that independent hardware requires massive capital. The market is consolidating around "The Big Three" (Meta, Apple, Google/Samsung).
