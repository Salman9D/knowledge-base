## Leadership Validation (May 2026)

At the May 05 debrief with Moiz, the opportunity framework was stress-tested against a B2B-first, data-moat thesis. Key additions from that session:

- **Data moat is the selection filter**: An opportunity is only viable if it can generate proprietary task/scene data that competitors cannot acquire. Generic assistants do not qualify.
- **Pilot access strategy**: 9D should offer no-cost builds to enterprise partners in exchange for exclusive data access. The first vertical should have accessible local partners (initially Pakistan market).
- **Refined vertical shortlist**: Industrial training (factory floors), data center operations, labs, construction — all named as meeting the three criteria: high-frequency glasses usage, clear ROI, and data moat potential.
- **B2C calorie-tracker POC**: Paused indefinitely. Not a strategic fit under the new thesis.

## The Opportunity Ranking (Full 10-Point Set)

The following opportunities are ranked by their ability to provide high-value outcomes with low capital intensity and clear paths from mobile/web to glasses/headsets.

### 1. AI Visual Workflow Copilot (Field Teams)
- **Rank**: Highest
- **Logic**: Mobile-first visual assistant for technicians and inspectors. Uses camera input for step-by-step guidance and issue detection.
- **Strategic Value**: Solves clear enterprise pain; leverages AI strength; doesn't require a headset to start.

### 2. Mobile-First Immersive Training Builder
- **Rank**: Highest
- **Logic**: Fast-to-build training modules for retail, safety, and healthcare support. Focuses on content generation speed via AI.
- **Strategic Value**: High TAM ($16B); converts well to a SaaS + services model.

### 3. Vertical Navigation & Context Assistant
- **Rank**: High
- **Logic**: Narrow assistants for specific environments (hospitals, airports, campuses). Aligns with high (39%) AR navigation CAGR.
- **Strategic Value**: Strong demand for practical utility; clear "glasses-native" future.

### 4. AR Commerce & Product Guidance Platform
- **Rank**: High
- **Logic**: Niche visualization for high-value verticals (Industrial equipment, Medical devices, Luxury goods).
- **Strategic Value**: Clear monetization; high growth (32% CAGR).

### 5. Spatial Collaboration for Design & Review
- **Rank**: Medium-High
- **Logic**: Specific review tools for AEC (Architecture, Engineering, Construction) rather than generic "metaverse offices."
- **Strategic Value**: Strong ROI for professional teams; validated by Apple Vision Pro enterprise focus.

### 6. XR Trust, Safety, and Compliance Layer
- **Rank**: Medium-High
- **Logic**: Middleware for visible consent, audit logging, and role-based spatial data access.
- **Strategic Value**: Addresses the #1 adoption barrier (Privacy); sells as enterprise security software.

### 7. Education & Tutoring Assistant (Spatial Overlays)
- **Rank**: Medium-High
- **Logic**: Phone/tablet-first tutor that explains objects, science labs, or vocational tasks in real space.
- **Strategic Value**: Strong AI fit; extends easily to smart glasses.

### 8. Industrial Knowledge Graph / Persistent Spatial Notes
- **Rank**: Medium
- **Logic**: Teams "pin" notes and warnings to physical assets, creating operational memory.
- **Strategic Value**: Highly differentiated and defensible; requires strong identity/UX management.

### 9. Sector-Specific Simulation Products
- **Rank**: Medium
- **Logic**: Packaged simulations for high-risk drills (Emergency response, Aviation ground training).
- **Strategic Value**: Strong vertical fit; higher content production cost than generalized platforms.

### 10. Platform-Adjacent Developer Tools
- **Rank**: Medium
- **Logic**: Software tools for asset management, AI scene labeling, or test automation for Android XR/visionOS.
- **Strategic Value**: Platform-agnostic upside; requires winning developer mindshare.
