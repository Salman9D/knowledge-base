## The Trust Gap (2026 Data)

As AI-powered smart glasses and headsets become more pervasive, consumer and enterprise concern regarding privacy has reached a critical threshold.

- **Data Privacy**: **70%** of US consumers worry about data privacy when using digital services.
- **AI Misuse**: **82%** of consumers worry about the potential misuse of generative AI.
- **Social Backlash**: Smart glasses are triggering public concern regarding covert recording and unconsented data collection.

## Opportunity: The Compliance Layer

Addressing these concerns is not just a feature; it is a **Ranked #4 Opportunity** for 9D Technologies to build a defensible software middleware.

### Core Product Capabilities
1. **Visible Consent UX**: Digital indicators and "handshake" protocols that make recording or data collection transparent to bystanders.
2. **Role-Based Spatial Access**: Controlling what data is captured (and where) based on employee role and physical zone (e.g., blurring sensitive patient info in hospitals).
3. **Audit Logging & Policy Management**: Centralized console for enterprise IT to enforce "no-go" zones and data retention policies for HUD sessions.
4. **Biometric Privacy**: On-device processing of eye-tracking and facial data to ensure it never leaves the local hardware.

## Strategic Significance

For industries like **Healthcare**, **Defense**, and **Legal**, trust and safety are the primary blockers to deployment. A company that provides a "Privacy-First SDK" can sell into these high-value verticals as a trusted broker, even while using third-party hardware from Meta or Google.
