# B2B Idea Scoring - Local (Pakistan)

**Summary**: A brutally honest evaluation of B2B Spatial AI ideas based on the specific constraints of the Pakistani market (corruption, infrastructure, data secrecy, and digital literacy).

**Sources**: [[b2b-eval-criteria-L-raza]], [[pakistan-b2b-ideas-database]], [[pakistan-adoption-and-moat-analysis]]

**Last updated**: 2026-05-15

---

## Evaluation Methodology

Ideas are scored from 1-10 based on three categories, through the lens of Pakistani industrial realities:
1.  **Quality of Idea**: Theoretical value and problem-solution fit.
2.  **Moat**: Defensibility through localized data and switching costs.
3.  **Adoption (Critical)**: Ease of implementation considering "gritty realities" (bribery, power, literacy).

---

## 1. Top Tier (Pilot Candidates)

### Surgical Instrument QC & Compliance (Sialkot)
*   **Quality**: 9/10
*   **Moat**: 10/10 (Proprietary visual data of Sialkot-specific defects)
*   **Adoption**: 7/10
*   **Critical Critique**: Strongest use case. **Downside**: High resistance from veteran manual polishers who view AI as a threat to their job security. Adoption requires "selling" it as a tool for export protection, not replacement.
*   **Adoption Blocker**: Corruption in international certification audits might make "automated compliance" a threat to existing informal "arrangements."

### Textile Quality Control & Defect Detection (Faisalabad)
*   **Quality**: 8/10
*   **Moat**: 9/10 (Localized fabric types/recycled cotton signatures)
*   **Adoption**: 6/10
*   **Critical Critique**: Massive market, but mills are extremely secretive about their operational inefficiencies due to tax evasion fears.
*   **Adoption Blocker**: Infrastructure. High-speed loom vision requires consistent power; mill owners may refuse to invest in the UPS/Solar backup needed for the AI sensors.

---

## 2. Mid Tier (High Potential, High Friction)

### Construction BIM Deviation Tracker
*   **Quality**: 9/10
*   **Moat**: 8/10
*   **Adoption**: 4/10
*   **Critical Critique**: **Total adoption failure likely**. The "gritty reality" of construction in Pakistan involves massive "deviation" from approved maps for cost-cutting or bribery.
*   **Adoption Blocker**: Contractors *want* the deviation. A transparent AI tracker that flags discrepancies is a direct threat to their "savings" and existing informal deals with building inspectors.

### Pharma Lab GMP Compliance
*   **Quality**: 9/10
*   **Moat**: 10/10
*   **Adoption**: 5/10
*   **Critical Critique**: High-value data moat. **Downside**: Extremely slow sales cycle (1-2 years) due to heavy government regulation (DRAP).
*   **Adoption Blocker**: Government gatekeepers. Securing "regulatory approval" for an automated compliance system will likely involve unofficial "facilitation fees" (bribery) which complicates corporate governance.

---

## 3. Low Tier (High Risk of Failure)

### Brick-Kiln Output Monitoring
*   **Quality**: 7/10
*   **Moat**: 7/10
*   **Adoption**: 2/10
*   **Critical Critique**: **Avoid**. This industry is largely informal, uses bonded labor, and operates almost entirely outside the tax net.
*   **Adoption Blocker**: Owners will view camera-based monitoring as a direct tax-authority tool. Zero trust environment.

### Solar Health Monitor (Net-Metering)
*   **Quality**: 8/10
*   **Moat**: 6/10
*   **Adoption**: 4/10
*   **Critical Critique**: Good tech, but the DISCOs (power companies) are actively hostile to net-metering.
*   **Adoption Blocker**: Any tool that makes solar more efficient might be met with regulatory pushback or "lost paperwork" at the utility company level to protect their revenue.
