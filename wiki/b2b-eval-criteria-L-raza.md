# B2B Evaluation Criteria - Local (Pakistan)

**Summary**: A detailed brainstorm of the practical, technical, and cultural limitations and opportunities for AR/VR B2B solutions in the Pakistani market.

**Sources**: [[spatial-ai-sync-2026-05-07]], [[pakistan-adoption-and-moat-analysis]], User Request

**Last updated**: 2026-05-15

---

## 1. Challenges & Implementation Blockers

Successfully deploying B2B solutions in Pakistan requires navigating a complex layer of informal and structural barriers.

### A. Corruption & Bribery (Informal Costs)
*   **The Issue**: Dealing with "bhatta" (protection money) or the necessity to grease palms to secure government tenders or large industrial approvals.
*   **Impact**: Increases the "hidden" cost of sales and complicates legal compliance for companies aiming for transparent operations.
*   **Strategy**: Focus on private-sector industries (Sialkot, Faisalabad) where owner-led decision-making can bypass some bureaucratic gatekeepers.

### B. Secrecy & Data Resistance
*   **The Issue**: Many businesses are reluctant to share operational data due to fears of tax audits or theft of trade secrets (unregistered intellectual property).
*   **Impact**: Makes it extremely difficult to collect the "localized data" needed for the [[pakistan-adoption-and-moat-analysis]].
*   **Strategy**: Implement "Zero-Cloud" edge processing to reassure owners that their data never leaves their premises.

### C. Government & Regulatory Volatility
*   **The Issue**: Sudden import bans (to control dollar outflow) or 60%+ duties on high-end electronics like AR glasses.
*   **Impact**: Makes hardware-dependent business models (e.g., Apple Vision Pro fleet) financially unviable.
*   **Strategy**: Stick to [[tablet-first-strategy]] using locally available devices and focus on software that can be distributed digitally.

### D. Infrastructure & Power Issues
*   **The Issue**: Frequent load shedding and inconsistent internet in industrial SITE areas.
*   **Impact**: Real-time cloud-dependent spatial AI will fail during outages.
*   **Strategy**: Mandatory offline-first architecture with local sync capabilities.

### E. Digital Literacy Gaps
*   **The Issue**: High friction for the blue-collar workforce who may struggle with complex UI or English-only instructions.
*   **Impact**: Poor adoption leads to pilot failure.
*   **Strategy**: Use visual/spatial overlays (arrows, green/red highlights) and voice feedback in local languages (Urdu/Punjabi).

---

## 2. Positive Aspects & Opportunities

Despite the challenges, the Pakistani market offers unique strategic advantages for building a defensive moat.

### A. Leapfrog Tech Adoption
*   **Observation**: Many industries lack complex legacy digital systems (like SAP), allowing them to jump straight from paper-based workflows to Spatial AI without the "technical debt" of middle-ware.

### B. Cheap Labor for Data Collection
*   **Observation**: The cost of manual annotation and visual data gathering is significantly lower than in global markets. This can be used to build high-quality, localized training sets for 1/10th of the global cost.

### C. Strong Informal Networks
*   **Observation**: Industry clusters in Pakistan (e.g., surgical tools in Sialkot) operate on strong trust-based networks. A single successful pilot can lead to rapid, word-of-mouth adoption across an entire sector.
