# What Exists Today? Spatial AI Assistants Landscape (April 2026)

## Overview
Spatial AI assistants aim to combine real-world perception, continuous context, low-friction interface, multimodal reasoning, and cross-app action.

While no single product fully realizes this, segments have made notable advances.

---

## Updated Landscape

### 1. Google Lens + Gemini-Powered Smart Glasses
- **Google Lens:** Widely used camera-based visual search integrating recognition, OCR, translation, and simple action bridging.
- **Google Gemini Glasses:** Launch expected in 2026 with AI assistant capabilities powered by Gemini; collaborations like Warby Parker aim for lightweight AI-powered specs.
- Market sources:
  - [Interesting Engineering](https://interestingengineering.com/ai-robotics/google-ai-smart-glasses-2026-launch)
  - [MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2025/12/08/google-ai-smart-glasses-2026/)
  - [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/warby-parker-google-launch-ai-powered-smart-glasses-2026-2025-12-08/)

### 2. Apple Intelligence + Vision Pro
- Vision Pro remains the premium spatial computing device with robust spatial UI and ecosystem hooks.
- Apple Intelligence layer provides multi-modal assistance integrated tightly with device hardware and software. 
- Rumored Apple smart glasses with cameras and Siri integration expected to complement Vision Pro.
- Market sources:
  - [MacWorld](https://www.macworld.com/article/3117710/apple-smart-glasses-release-date-specs-features-price.html)
  - Apple official resources and news

### 3. Meta AI on Ray-Ban Meta Glasses
- Meta Ray-Ban AI-powered glasses provide persistent always-available voice AI in a light wearable.
- Includes discreet display, Transitions lenses, and companion Neural Band offering extended battery life.
- Early ambient assistant with limitations in output modalities and deeper multi-step agency.
- Market sources:
  - [Meta.com](https://www.meta.com/ai-glasses/meta-ray-ban-display/)

### 4. Rabbit R1
- AI-first consumer device aimed at executing cross-app workflows with voice interaction.
- Operating system focused on action modeling (LAM) to infer and replicate user behavior.
- Mixed reviews on reliability and practical utility, shares conceptual space with Humane AI Pin.
- Market sources:
  - [The Verge review](https://www.theverge.com/2024/5/2/24147159/rabbit-r1-review-ai-gadget)
  - [Rabbit.tech](https://www.rabbit.tech/rabbit-r1)

### 5. Humane AI Pin
- Wearable ambient AI device with voice and projected display interaction.
- Ambition for phone-free AI access but challenged by poor execution and user acceptance.

### 6. AskUI
- Software agent layer focusing on AI-driven UI control and automation.
- Currently desktop/web focused with no physical embodiment.
- Highlights need for spatial assistants to act on software as well as perceive reality.

### 7. Emerging Platforms & Technologies
- OpenAI multimodal agents and Google DeepMind research powering future intelligence layers.
- Samsung and Qualcomm working on hardware/SDK enabling AI-powered glasses.

---

## Gaps & Challenges
- No winner yet spanning perception, persistent memory, seamless interface, and agency.
- Lightweight wearables struggle against bulky headsets and limited power.
- Privacy, trust, and social norms challenge constant spatial AI presence.

## Outlook
The spatial AI assistant space is poised for accelerated advances in 2026, driven by multimodal AI progress and hardware maturation. Key watchpoints include:
- Google/Warby Parker launching Gemini glass products
- Apple smart glasses complementing Vision Pro
- Meta refining Ray-Ban AI capabilities
- Improved AI models powering both perception and agentic behaviors
