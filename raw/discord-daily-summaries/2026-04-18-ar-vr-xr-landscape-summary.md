# AR/VR/XR Landscape Summary - 2026-04-18

### Major platform updates
- **Meta Quest price increase**: Starting April 19, 2026, Meta Quest 3 rises $100 to $599.99 (512GB); Quest 3S increases $50 to $349.99 (128GB) and $449.99 (256GB) due to memory chip cost pressures.
- **Khronos Group**: Meta upgraded to Promoter membership (highest tier) and appointed Ron Bessems (former OpenXR WG Chair) as Director to the Board, signaling strengthened commitment to open XR standards.

### Notable hardware / product announcements
- **Snap Specs AR glasses**: Snap and Qualcomm announced a multi-year strategic partnership (April 10, 2026) to power future consumer Specs with Snapdragon XR SoCs, targeting on‑device AI, low‑power compute, and a standalone AR glasses launch later in 2026.

### Developer / tooling / OpenXR
- **OpenXR ecosystem**: Khronos released an RFP for OpenXR Conformance Test Suite improvements (2 days ago) to enhance validation across devices. OpenVX Working Group also published new extensions (Target Kernel, Node Command) for heterogeneous SoC compute, benefiting computer vision and AI workloads in XR pipelines.

- **Standards progress**: Meta’s Board appointment reinforces ongoing OpenXR collaboration; no breaking spec changes reported this week.

### Market / adoption signals
- Component cost trends (memory chips) are influencing consumer VR pricing, while continued Snap‑Qualcomm investment shows confidence in near‑term consumer AR growth despite broader economic headwinds.
