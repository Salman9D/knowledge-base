# AR/VR/XR Landscape Summary - 2026-03-18

## Summary

The clearest fresh XR development today is **Meta’s decision to remove Horizon Worlds from Quest and end VR access entirely by June 15, 2026**. That looks less like a one-off app sunset and more like a broader platform refocus: Quest remains the VR ecosystem, while Horizon Worlds becomes a mobile/web social product.

Outside Meta, the most useful recent signals are:
- **Samsung** giving fresh detail on AI smart glasses tied to its Android XR ecosystem.
- **Pico** continuing to position Project Swan + Pico OS 6 as a meaningful spatial-computing challenger.
- **Khronos** sustaining interoperability and 3D pipeline work, especially around OpenXR and glTF.
- **Market momentum** shifting toward smart glasses and recurring content monetization rather than metaverse-style social VR.

## 1. Major platform updates

- **Meta is formally removing Horizon Worlds from Quest.**  
  Meta’s official Quest announcement says Horizon Worlds and Events will stop appearing in the Quest Store by **March 31, 2026**, and the app will be removed from Quest on **June 15, 2026**. Worlds will continue as a mobile experience via the Meta Horizon app.

- **Strategic implication:**  
  Meta is increasingly treating **Quest as a VR app/game platform** and **Horizon Worlds as a separate mobile-first social platform**. That reduces internal platform confusion, but it is also an admission that the VR-first version of Horizon Worlds did not become a core Quest driver.

- **Apple / Google status:**  
  No major same-day high-signal official Apple Vision Pro or Google Android XR platform release surfaced from the tracked source set during this pass. Google’s most recent visible XR activity remains partner demos and ecosystem signaling rather than a fresh platform launch today.

## 2. Notable hardware / product announcements

- **Samsung smart glasses are getting more concrete.**  
  In recent CNBC reporting from MWC, Samsung described smartphone-connected AI smart glasses with an eye-level camera and said it wants something available for industry this year. This strengthens the Android XR stack beyond headsets.

- **Pico Project Swan remains worth tracking.**

Pico recently outlined late-2026 ambitions for a flagship XR headset with **MicroOLED**, high claimed PPI/PPD, and a new **Pico OS 6** stack. It is still early, but among challenger platforms this is one of the more substantial hardware/platform combinations on the board.

- **VITURE continues to attract funding.**  
  VITURE added another **$100M** round, bringing it past **$200M raised in six months**. That signals continuing investor belief in lightweight XR glasses as a category.

## 3. Developer / tooling / OpenXR

- **OpenXR interoperability remains strategically important.**  
  Khronos’ recent OpenXR-focused material emphasizes practical pathways for interoperable XR development, especially in European project ecosystems. Not a headline-grabber, but still one of the healthiest long-term themes in XR.

- **glTF Gaussian Splatting matters for spatial pipelines.**  
  Khronos’ release-candidate extension for Gaussian Splatting in glTF is relevant for photoreal capture, digital twins, and scene-reconstruction workflows that increasingly feed XR experiences.

- **Pico’s developer story is improving.**  
  Pico’s recent briefing highlighted a **Spatial SDK (Kotlin)**, componentized UI APIs, and a system architecture aimed at blending Android-style apps with immersive interfaces. If executed well, that lowers friction for Android-oriented XR teams.

## 4. Market / adoption signals

- **Meta’s Horizon retreat is itself a market signal.**  
  The company is cutting VR support for its flagship social-worlds product while continuing to invest in Quest broadly. That suggests the company sees more durable value in third-party VR apps/games than in first-party social VR worlds.

- **Quest monetization looks steadier than Quest social.**  
  Road to VR reports **Horizon+** has passed **1 million active members**. If accurate, that points to subscription/content monetization becoming a more stable part of the Quest business.

- **Smart glasses remain the strongest consumer-scale signal.**  
  Road to VR, citing EssilorLuxottica’s Q4 2025 results, reports **7M+ smart glasses sold in 2025** with Meta partnerships. That is one of the clearest signs that lighter wearable form factors are landing better than most VR growth narratives.

- **Capital is still flowing into XR glasses.**  
  VITURE’s funding round reinforces that investors are still willing to back the category, especially where products look closer to everyday eyewear than full headsets.

## 6. Actionable takeaway

The most practical takeaway today is that **XR platform strategy is consolidating around what actually scales**:

- VR headsets remain strongest for dedicated apps and games.
- Social/world-building is increasingly shifting to **mobile-first**.
- Smart glasses are becoming the **mainstream adoption wedge**.
- Developers should keep betting on **portable standards and flexible content pipelines** rather than any one vendor’s “all-in” world vision.

If you’re building or investing, the safer near-term posture is:
- prioritize **OpenXR / interoperable pipelines** where possible,
- watch **Android XR + Samsung** closely,
- treat **smart glasses** as the highest-momentum adjacent category,
- and read Meta’s Horizon move as a signal to be skeptical of VR-native social as the primary growth story.
