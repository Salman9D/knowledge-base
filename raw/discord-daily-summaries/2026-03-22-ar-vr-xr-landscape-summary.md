# AR/VR/XR Landscape Summary - 2026-03-22

## Major platform updates
- **Meta reverses Horizon Worlds shutdown**: After planned June 15 VR discontinuation, Meta confirmed Quest support remains active while prioritizing mobile development (March 19 announcement via Glass Almanac). Quest access preserved amid declining headset sales (-16% YoY) but growing mobile traction (1.5M downloads in 2026).
- **Apple Vision Pro chip upgrade**: M5 Pro and M5 Max chips now power Vision Pro, bringing 18-core CPU (with super cores) and up to 40-core GPU with Neural Accelerators—over 4x AI compute and up to 35% better ray tracing graphics vs prior generation (Apple Newsroom, March 3).

## Notable hardware / product announcements
- **Pico OS 6 & Project Swan**: Pico unveiled OS 6 with PanoScreen for 360° multitasking and teased Project Swan, targeting work-focused spatial computing with OpenXR/WebXR/Unity/Unreal support (Glass Almanac, March 15).
- **Xreal Neo dock canceled**: Xreal halted its Neo dock update on March 13, signaling a hardware rethink for mobile AR as competitors shift focus (Glass Almanac).
- **Samsung/Google Android XR push**: Both companies advancing Android XR and Galaxy XR initiatives to create lower-cost Vision Pro alternatives (Glass Almanac).

## Developer / tooling / OpenXR
- **Pico OS 6 emphasizes open toolkits**: Explicit support for OpenXR, WebXR, Unity, and Unreal in Pico’s new OS, aiming to lower barriers for spatial app development (Glass Almanac).
- **Vision Pro gains M5 chip benefits**: Developers targeting Vision Pro now access substantially improved GPU performance for ray tracing and AI workloads via M5 Pro/Max (Apple Newsroom).

## Market / adoption signals
- **Steam Spring Sale live**: Over 1,000 VR games discounted until March 26 at 1pm ET, indicating sustained consumer VR engagement despite platform shifts (UploadVR, March 21).
- **Meta’s mobile pivot**: Horizon Worlds mobile downloads accelerating (1.5M in 2026) while Quest sales decline, validating Meta’s shift toward mobile-first XR strategy (Glass Almanac/Times of India).
