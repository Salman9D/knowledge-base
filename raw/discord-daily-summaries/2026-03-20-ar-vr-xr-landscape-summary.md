# AR/VR/XR Landscape Summary

## 1. Major platform updates

- **Meta partially reversed its Horizon Worlds VR shutdown plan.** After officially saying Quest VR access would end on June 15, Meta CTO Andrew Bosworth said existing VR worlds will remain accessible “for the foreseeable future,” while new development remains focused on mobile.
- This does **not** look like a VR revival. The stronger interpretation is a **legacy-support concession** after user pushback, while Meta continues moving Horizon toward mobile/web usage.
- From the tracked source set, there were **no equally strong same-day Apple, Google, or OpenXR platform updates** worth elevating.

## 2. Notable hardware / product announcements

- **No major trustworthy same-day hardware launch or headset announcement** stood out from the tracked source set.
- The most material product-level changes today were service/ecosystem changes inside Meta’s Quest stack:
  - Horizon Worlds and Events removed from Quest Store by **March 31, 2026**
  - Hyperscape Capture viewing moved out of Horizon Worlds by **March 24, 2026**
  - Horizon-specific Meta Horizon Plus perks removed by **March 31, 2026**

- Overall signal: **ecosystem consolidation**, not expansion.

## 3. Developer / tooling / OpenXR

- **No fresh OpenXR-specific standard update** surfaced today from Khronos that looked headline-worthy.
- Meta’s developer signal is clearer:
  - **Legacy Unity-runtime Horizon worlds** appear to retain VR access
  - **New Horizon Engine development** remains mobile-focused
- For developers, this implies:
  - legacy VR content may remain supportable
  - net-new investment inside Horizon should be evaluated as **mobile-first**
  - Horizon is becoming a weaker bet as a VR-native growth platform
- Adjacent tooling note: Khronos’ Vulkan news stream this week includes **Roadmap 2026 / descriptor heap work** and a new engine tutorial. This is not OpenXR news, but it is relevant to immersive rendering stacks.

## 4. Market / adoption signals

- The biggest market signal today is **Meta’s strategic posture**:

- planned VR shutdown
  - backlash
  - partial rollback
  - ongoing mobile-first emphasis
- TechCrunch cited Appfigures data indicating Horizon Worlds’ mobile app reached **45 million total downloads** and **1.5 million downloads so far in 2026**.
- Practical implication: **mobile still appears to have materially better traction than VR social worlds**, and Meta is aligning resources around that reality.

## 6. Actionable takeaway

- **For strategy:** today reinforces that VR social platforms remain fragile unless they demonstrate clear user pull.
- **For developers:** treat Meta Horizon as **mobile-led with legacy VR support**, not as a growing VR-first platform.
- **For monitoring next:** watch for

- a formal Meta support matrix for legacy VR worlds
  - Quest Store discoverability changes after March 31
  - any fresh Apple, Google, or Khronos XR platform updates that materially shift the near-term landscape
