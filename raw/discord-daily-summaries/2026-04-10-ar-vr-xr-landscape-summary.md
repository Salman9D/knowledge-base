# AR/VR/XR Landscape Summary

## Major platform updates

- **Google / Android XR** rolled out a meaningful April update for Samsung Galaxy XR. Key additions include:
  - Android Enterprise support
  - auto-spatialization for turning 2D apps, websites, images, and videos into 3D
  - app pinning to walls
  - visible real hands in home space mode
  - session restore
- Google also says Android XR now has **100+ immersive apps**, more than doubling since launch.

Sources:
- https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/platforms/android/android-xr-immersive-features-update-april-2026/
- https://www.roadtovr.com/android-xr-update-samsung-galaxy-xr-enterprise/

- **Apple / visionOS** has no major same-day public XR launch, but Apple’s latest official XR-relevant signals remain:

- **visionOS 26.5 beta** availability from March 30
  - updated Apple Developer Program terms that explicitly mention the **Foveated Streaming framework**
- For developers, that terms update is the more interesting signal because it suggests foveated streaming is moving into a more formalized deployment posture.

Source:
- https://developer.apple.com/news/

## Notable hardware / product announcements

- **Meta** introduced its first **prescription-optimized AI glasses**:
  - Ray-Ban Meta Blayzer Optics (Gen 2)
  - Ray-Ban Meta Scriber Optics (Gen 2)
- US pre-orders start at **$499**, with optical retailer availability beginning April 14.
- This matters because it targets a much larger everyday-wear audience instead of just early adopters willing to retrofit lenses.

Source:
- https://about.fb.com/news/2026/03/meta-ai-glasses-built-for-prescriptions/

## Developer / tooling / OpenXR

- **Khronos** expanded the **OpenXR Extension Matrix** to include middleware-level support data, not just runtime support.
- Initial middleware participants include:
  - Godot
  - LÖVR
  - StereoKit
  - Unity
  - Unreal Engine
- This gives developers a clearer picture of where extension support breaks across engines and runtimes.

Source:
- https://www.khronos.org/blog/khronos-adds-middleware-insights-to-the-openxr-extension-matrix

- **Meta and Unity** extended their multi-year partnership around XR platform support.
- The practical takeaway is continued Unity support for Meta’s XR stack, which still matters because Unity remains one of the dominant VR development pipelines.

Source:
- https://www.roadtovr.com/meta-extends-commitment-xr-development-meta/

- **Valve** released a native **Steam Link beta for visionOS**.
- Important limitation: it supports **2D streaming only**, not VR content yet.
- Even so, it improves Vision Pro’s utility as a high-end spatial display for PC gaming.

Sources:
- https://steamcommunity.com/app/353380/discussions/7/806847328212494915/
- https://www.roadtovr.com/valve-steam-link-beta-vision-pro-vr-games/

## Market / adoption signals

- Google says Android XR now supports **100+ immersive apps**, more than 2x growth since Galaxy XR launched.
- That is still early, but it is one of the few concrete official ecosystem-growth datapoints this week.

Source:

- https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/platforms/android/android-xr-immersive-features-update-april-2026/

- **Rec Room** is shutting down on **June 1, 2026**, despite once reaching **150M+ players** and a **$3.5B valuation**.
- This is a strong market signal that social XR engagement alone has not translated into durable platform economics.

## Actionable takeaway

The clearest pattern right now is **practical maturation over hype**:

- Android XR is improving where enterprises and everyday workflows care most.
- Meta is broadening the smart-glasses funnel through prescription-first design.
- Khronos is reducing developer uncertainty around OpenXR implementation gaps.
- At the same time, the Rec Room shutdown is a reminder that adoption without strong economics is still fragile.

If prioritizing near-term tracking, the best watch items are:
- Android XR enterprise deployment traction
- prescription AI glasses sell-through and repeat usage
- whether the improved OpenXR matrix actually reduces cross-platform shipping friction
