# AR/VR/XR Landscape Summary - 2026-03-24

## Major Platform Updates
- Android XR launched at Google I/O 2026 as a re-architected Android OS for spatial computing, featuring native OpenXR 1.1 implementation, low-latency display compositor, and deep Google AI integration
- Meta reversed Horizon Worlds VR shutdown (March 19) - will continue Quest support while refocusing Horizon Worlds on mobile, signaling a pivot toward wearables and AI glasses
- Apple announced WWDC 2026 for June 8-12, with new hardware including Studio Display, Studio Display XDR, and MacBook Air with M5 chip
- Apple Vision Pro production scaled back due to weak sales, with resources shifting toward anticipated AR smart glasses for 2026/2027

## Notable Hardware/Product Announcements
- Samsung confirmed 2026 smart glasses based on Android XR, competing with Meta; leaked specs show 245 mAh battery suggesting display inclusion
- Samsung Galaxy XR headset specs: Dual 3.5K Micro-OLED (~40 PPD), pancake lenses (110° FoV), 16GB RAM, dual 12MP passthrough cameras, 6DoF tracking with eye tracking
- Lenovo launching enterprise Android XR headset with ergonomic design, ANSI Z87.1 safety rating, and focus on productivity/industrial use cases
- Warby Parker + Google rumblings for fashionable, prescription-ready AR glasses in 2026
- Amazon developing AR glasses focused on shopping convenience and hands-free retail assistance
- Snap established Specs subsidiary (Jan 2026) to sell lightweight consumer AR glasses at scale

## Developer/Tooling/OpenXR Changes
- Android XR provides native OpenXR 1.1 implementation (not just a loader), ensuring maximum performance and cross-vendor compatibility
- Developer toolchain: Android Studio XR Emulator, OpenXR SDK 1.1, Unity PolySpatial for cross-platform deployment, Unreal Engine 5.5 with Snapdragon Game Super Resolution
- World-scale AR: Google Cloud Anchors API for persistence, Niantic Lightship VPS and ARCore Geospatial Creator for city-scale experiences
- Open standards: Adoption of Open Universal Scene Description (OpenUSD) for spatial data, system-wide foveated rendering via Vulkan 1.3, forward-looking volumetric display support
- Qualcomm Spaces XR Platform offers optional hardware-accelerated SDK for features like plane detection and spatial meshing

## Market/Adoption Signals
- Global AR market projected at $210.96B for 2026, indicating rapid growth into consumer devices

- 58% of users bought something after using AR shopping tools, showing clear retail upside
- Horizon Worlds: 45M total downloads with +53% YoY increase in early 2026, demonstrating mobile traction
- Meta shifting funding from Metaverse toward AI glasses - planning ultralight Horizon OS headset with tethered compute puck for 2026, possibly delaying traditional Quest until 2027
- Xreal canceled Neo Switch 2 dock over battery/reliability concerns, narrowing gaming use cases for AR glasses
- Nearby Glasses app (Android) alerts users to nearby smart glasses, fueling privacy debates and pushing for recording indicators
