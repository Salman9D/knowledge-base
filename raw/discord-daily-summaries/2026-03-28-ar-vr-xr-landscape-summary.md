# AR/VR/XR Landscape Summary - 2026-03-28

## Summary

Today’s high-signal XR picture is less about a single blockbuster platform announcement and more about where momentum is actually concentrating:

- **Consumer momentum:** smart glasses still look stronger than dedicated XR headsets.
- **Enterprise momentum:** Apple Vision Pro is increasingly being used as a high-end visualization client connected to workstation/cloud rendering.
- **Developer momentum:** OpenXR remains the most credible route to lower fragmentation, especially for spatial computing features that need persistence and interoperability.
- **Content momentum:** VR software cadence remains active across Quest, PS VR2, and PC VR, with the Spring 2026 VR Games Showcase reinforcing that the ecosystem is still shipping.

## 1. Major platform updates

- Apple’s tracked developer news feed did not surface a fresh XR-specific platform announcement this week, but **WWDC26 is officially set for June 8–12**, making it the next likely milestone for **visionOS** news and tooling updates.
- Recent Vision Pro momentum continues to center on **visionOS 26.4** enabling **PC VR foveated streaming**, which now appears to be translating into real enterprise software usage rather than experimental demos alone.
- No equally strong fresh official Meta Quest or Android XR platform update surfaced in this pull from the tracked source set.

## 2. Notable hardware / product announcements

- **Meta / Ray-Ban smart glasses:** FCC filings indicate two apparent new models, **Ray-Ban Meta Scriber** and **Ray-Ban Meta Blazer**, suggesting another near-term hardware refresh.
- **VR Games Showcase (Spring 2026):** a large wave of software announcements and release-date confirmations landed across Quest, PS VR2, and PC VR. This is more ecosystem maintenance than market reset, but still important.
- **Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 on PS VR2:** the PS VR2 mode is slated for the **free Sim 5 update in April**, giving PS VR2 one of its more meaningful upcoming high-fidelity software additions.

## 3. Developer / tooling / OpenXR

- **Autodesk VRED + Apple Vision Pro + NVIDIA CloudXR** is the standout tooling story in this pull. VRED can now stream immersive sessions to Vision Pro while the heavy compute stays on a workstation or cloud PC.
- The implementation supports **real-time design changes**, **colocated collaboration**, and **3D annotation**, which makes it materially more relevant than a one-off showroom demo.
- **OpenXR** remains the cleanest long-term interoperability story. Khronos continues to emphasize reducing fragmentation through OpenXR 1.1 and by advancing spatial-computing-related extensions.
- The **OpenXR Spatial Entities extensions** remain especially important to watch for MR developers because they target:
  - plane and marker detection,
  - precise spatial anchors,
  - cross-session persistence.

## 4. Market / adoption signals

- The clearest consumer-market signal in today’s reading is still **smart glasses**: Road to VR cites **EssilorLuxottica** saying Meta smart glasses sold **over 7 million units last year**, reinforcing that lightweight wearable AI/glasses products currently have more obvious scale than VR headsets.
- On the enterprise side, **Kia, BMW, Volvo, and Rivian** are already cited as using the new **Autodesk VRED immersive mode for Vision Pro**, which is a stronger adoption indicator than generic “enterprise interest.”
- The software pipeline remains active: the **Spring 2026 VR Games Showcase** showed ongoing multi-platform support across Quest, PS VR2, PC VR, and related ecosystems.

## 6. Practical takeaway

The most actionable read right now:

- **Consumer XR:** watch **smart glasses** more closely than headset launches for mainstream adoption signals.
- **Enterprise XR:** Vision Pro’s most credible current path is as a **premium display/client for streamed workstation-class software**.
- **Developer strategy:** prioritize **cross-platform architectures** and keep watching **OpenXR** for the pieces that make persistent MR experiences more portable.
- **Near-term calendar watch:** **WWDC26** is the next major event likely to shift the Apple side of the XR landscape.
