# AR/VR/XR Landscape Summary - 2026-03-12

## Summary

Today’s live scan did not surface a major same-day Apple/Meta/Google platform announcement from the tracked source set. The highest-signal items are instead:
- Valve’s Steam Frame still appears on track for 2026, now labeled “coming soon” on Steam backend reporting.
- SteamVR’s February share drop looks more like survey-composition distortion than a clean demand collapse.
- OpenXR remains the strongest practical interoperability story, with Khronos conformance and CTS maintenance activity continuing in the background.

## 1. Major platform updates

- No major same-day official Apple/Meta/Google platform release note surfaced in the live scan.
- Khronos’ OpenXR conformance data remains the most useful platform-layer update:
  - Meta Quest 3 and Quest 3S are listed with OpenXR 1.1 conformance entries dated 2026-01-18 on Horizon OS v83.
  - Google Android XR / Galaxy XR remains visible in the conformant-products list, reinforcing that Android XR is still part of the standards picture even without fresh official developer posts today.

## 2. Notable hardware / product announcements

- **Valve Steam Frame**
  - Road to VR reports Valve says Steam Frame is still shipping in 2026 and is now marked “coming soon” on Steam backend.
  - This is meaningful as a continuity signal, but not yet strong enough to treat as firm launch timing.
  - Pricing remains the biggest unresolved variable for how disruptive Steam Frame could become.

## 3. Developer / tooling / OpenXR

- **OpenXR conformance remains the real story**

- Khronos’ conformant-products page is still the cleanest live source for checking which devices/runtimes have moved beyond marketing claims into standards compliance.
  - Notable listed entries include:
    - Meta Quest 3 — OpenXR 1.0 and 1.1
    - Meta Quest 3S — OpenXR 1.0 and 1.1
    - Google Android XR / Galaxy XR
    - NVIDIA CloudXR Runtime for Apple Vision Pro
- **OpenXR CTS 2026**
  - Khronos’ 2026 CTS RFP shows ongoing budget and contractor support for keeping the OpenXR conformance test suite current.
  - That matters because standards health depends on test quality, not just spec documents.

## 4. Market / adoption signals

- **SteamVR usage dip in February is likely misleading**
  - UploadVR highlights that SteamVR’s share fell to 1.05% in February, but attributes most of the decline to the annual Chinese New Year surge in overall Steam usage.
  - That explanation is credible because Steam’s language mix shifted sharply toward Simplified Chinese during the same period.
  - Conclusion: February is noisy; March and April will be more useful for trend confirmation.
- **Software pipeline is still moving**

- UploadVR’s March release roundup suggests the XR software calendar is still active across Quest, PS VR2, and PC VR.
  - That is a modest but useful ecosystem-health indicator.

## 6. Actionable takeaway

- Watch **Steam Frame** for actual preorder, price, and developer-readiness signals rather than backend-label changes alone.
- For builders, **OpenXR remains the least-regret path** across the current XR landscape.
- For market reads, do **not** treat February SteamVR share as a standalone demand verdict; wait for normalization in the next survey cycle.
