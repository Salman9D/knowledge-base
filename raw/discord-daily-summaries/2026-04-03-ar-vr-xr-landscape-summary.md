# AR/VR/XR Landscape Summary

## Major platform updates

- No major same-day Apple Vision Pro, Android XR, or Khronos/OpenXR platform bulletin surfaced in live retrieval from the tracked source set.
- The clearest near-term platform signal is Meta continuing to widen its AI glasses stack rather than announcing a new headset/runtime change this week.
- PlayStation’s most relevant recent platform move is Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 confirming PS VR2 support via the upcoming free “Sim 5” update in April, with explicit mention of foveated rendering and controller-specific interaction redesign.

## Notable hardware / product announcements

- **Meta announced two new prescription-first Ray-Ban Meta styles**: Blayzer Optics and Scriber Optics, starting at $499 in the US.
- The practical significance is not a new XR capability layer, but a push toward all-day wearability and normalizing smart glasses as primary eyewear.
- Meta also added new color/lens options across Ray-Ban Meta and Oakley Meta lines, reinforcing its consumer-eyewear-first distribution strategy.
- **Zero Latency and CD Projekt Red announced a Cyberpunk 2077 free-roam VR experience** for warehouse-scale venues worldwide.

## Developer / tooling / OpenXR

- **Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 on PS VR2** is the most concrete technical developer update in this batch:
  - cockpit interactions were redesigned for Sense controllers
  - foveated rendering needed substantial tuning
  - frame duplication was used to hit required performance
- **Meta’s sEMG wristband/input work** is moving toward ecosystem-building:
  - six external research teams funded
  - focus areas include learning curves, adaptive systems, silent input, and assistive use
- **No fresh OpenXR release surfaced today** in retrieval; Khronos OpenXR 1.1 remains the latest major standards simplification milestone relevant to cross-platform XR development.

## Market / adoption signals

- Meta’s prescription-optimized Ray-Ban push is a meaningful adoption signal: it directly targets one of the biggest real-world blockers to smart-glasses usage frequency, namely comfort and everyday wearability.
- FitXR launching **Flow Studio** is a useful retention signal in VR fitness. The move is framed around users actively looking for stability and replacement options amid uncertainty around Supernatural.
- Zero Latency’s Cyberpunk partnership suggests branded location-based XR still has room when paired with recognizable entertainment IP and proven venue infrastructure.

## Actionable takeaway

The strongest signal today is **ecosystem hardening rather than breakthrough hardware**:

- Meta is reducing friction around everyday smart-glasses adoption.
- Sony/Asobo are showing technically demanding flat-first titles can still be meaningfully adapted to PS VR2.
- Meta continues to place long bets on post-controller wearable input.
- VR fitness and location-based XR still show viable demand where they preserve habits or attach to strong IP.

If prioritizing what matters most from a product or strategy perspective, watch **wearable comfort, input innovation, and repeat-use behavior** more closely than headline-chasing headset speculation.
