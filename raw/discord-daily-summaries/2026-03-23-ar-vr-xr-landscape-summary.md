# AR/VR/XR Landscape Summary - 2026-03-23

## Major platform updates
- Meta reversed its decision to discontinue Horizon Worlds VR support, confirming the app will remain available on Quest headsets until June 15, 2026 (mobile-only after). The Quest Store listing ends March 31, 2026.
- Android XR launches as an open spatial computing platform with native OpenXR 1.1 implementation, positioning itself as an alternative to Apple's visionOS and Meta's Quest ecosystem.

## Notable hardware / product announcements
- Samsung Galaxy XR headset: dual 3.5K micro-OLED panels (~40 PPD), eye tracking, Wi-Fi 7, Snapdragon XR3 SoC, targeting prosumer/media use cases.

- Lenovo XR headset: enterprise-focused with ~35 PPD, ANSI Z87.1 safety rating, top-strap design for weight distribution, and device management platform support.
- No Apple Vision Pro hardware updates reported today; Apple newsroom focused on Mac, iPhone, iPad, and accessory launches.

## Developer / tooling / OpenXR
- Android XR provides native OpenXR 1.1 runtime, ensuring high-performance, cross-vendor compatibility for OpenXR applications.
- Qualcomm Spaces XR Platform offers hardware-accelerated extensions (plane detection, image tracking, spatial meshing) tuned for Snapdragon XR3.
- First-class support in Unity via PolySpatial (cross-platform deployment) and Unreal Engine 5.5 via Snapdragon Game Super Resolution (GSR) for performance uplift.
- Open Universal Scene Description (OpenUSD) adopted as core format for scene geometry, enabling interoperable digital twins.
- System-level integration of Google Lens AI for real-time object recognition, text translation, and landmark detection.
- WebXR Device API support in Chrome for frictionless web-based AR/VR access.

## Market / adoption signals
- Steam's 2026 Spring Sale (live through March 26) offers discounts on over 1,000 VR games, indicating sustained consumer VR gaming interest.
- Horizon Worlds mobile app: 45 million total worldwide downloads (iOS + Google Play), with 1.5 million downloads in 2026 (53% year-over-year increase). However, total consumer spending remains low at $1.1 million.
- Meta's Reality Labs continues significant losses (~$73B since 2021) but has shifted focus to mobile AR after low VR engagement; Horizon Worlds VR support maintained due to user backlash.

## Actionable takeaway
For developers: Android XR offers an open, standardized platform with native OpenXR, web support, and enterprise flexibility—worth evaluating for new spatial computing projects. For consumers: Watch for Samsung Galaxy XR (prosumer) and Lenovo XR (enterprise) launches as credible alternatives to Vision Pro and Quest. For investors: Note Meta's strategic pivot to mobile AR and the growing Android XR ecosystem as a potential counterweight to walled garden platforms.
